1.
In each of the following sentences, there is one underlined word and one gap. From the list of words lettered A to D, choose the one that is most nearly opposite in meaning to the underlined word and that will at the same time, correctly fill the gap in the sentence.

While Mr. Ade is……, his son is a novice.

A Enlightened
B Competent
C Clever
D Experienced
The correct answer is Option D. Experienced.

Experienced: Means having knowledge or skill in a particular area, which is the opposite of "novice."

On the other hand,

A. Enlightened: Means having knowledge or insight, not directly related to experience.

Also,

B. Competent: Means having the necessary ability or skills, which is not directly opposite to "novice."

And lastly,

C. Clever: Means intelligent or quick to understand, not necessarily experienced.

2.
It is better to use persuasion rather than……to get witnesses to court.
A Effort
B Coercion
C Influence
D Pressure
The correct answer is Option B. Coercion.

The sentence reads: "It is better to use persuasion rather than ____ to get witnesses to court." To find the correct word, we need to identify a term that is nearly opposite in meaning to "persuasion." Persuasion involves convincing someone through reasoning or argument, typically in a gentle or positive manner. The opposite would be forcing someone to do something against their will.

B. Coercion: Means forcing someone to do something by using threats or pressure, which is opposite to persuasion.

On the other hand,

A. Effort: Means physical or mental energy used to do something, not opposite to persuasion.

Also,

C. Influence: Means the capacity to have an effect on someone, not opposite to persuasion.

And lastly,

D. Pressure: While related to coercion, is not as precise in conveying the opposite meaning of persuasion.

7.
There was an agitation for the….. law on libel to be re-enacted.
A Enforced
B Repealed
C Rejected
D Cancelled
The correct answer is Option B. Repealed.

B. Repealed: The sentence reads: "There was an agitation for the ____ law on libel to be re-enacted."

To understand this, consider:

  • Agitation: A state of public demand or protest.
  • Re-enacted: To pass a law again.

Repealed means the law was abolished or annulled. Re-enacting it means people want it back.

On the other hand,

A. Enforced: Means to compel compliance. But you can't enforce a law that no longer exists.

Also,

C. Rejected: Means refused or dismissed, not necessarily a law previously in existence.

And lastly,

D. Cancelled: Though close in meaning to repealed, "repealed" is the proper legal term for annulling laws.

A. Excellent: While this term does imply a high standard, it’s more subjective and less specific than “quality,” which has a more defined meaning in the context of construction materials.

B. Recommended: This could vary depending on who is making the recommendation. It doesn’t necessarily mean the materials are of good quality.

D. Exceptional: This term implies that the materials are extraordinary or superior in some way, which might not be necessary for standard construction. Quality materials, on the other hand, simply need to meet certain standards.
