1.

Instruction: In each of the following sentences, there is one underlined word and one gap. From the list of words lettered A to D, choose the one that is most nearly opposite in meaning to the underlined word and that will, at the same time, correctly fill the gap in the sentence.

The posh cars were sandwiched between two …..ones.

A dented
B rugged
C rickety
D modest
The correct answer is Option D. modest.

D. modest: This is the correct answer. "Modest" means simple or not luxurious, which is the opposite of "posh."

On the other hand,

A. dented: Suggests damaged or imperfect, but not the direct opposite of "posh."

Also,

B. rugged: Implies rough or sturdy — not directly opposite to "posh."

And lastly,

C. rickety: Means shaky or unstable, but not exactly the opposite of "posh."

On the other hand,

C. unbearable: Too strong a word, not a direct opposite of "trivial."

And lastly,
