1.
The by-product of fermentation of sugar is
A Carbon(IV) oxide
B Ethanoic acid
C Propanol
D Propan-1, 2, 3-triol
The correct answer is Option A. Carbon(IV) oxide.

A. Carbon(IV) oxide (Correct): During the fermentation process, yeast or bacteria convert sugars (such as glucose) into ethanol and carbon dioxide. The reaction can be summarized as:

C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂

This shows that carbon dioxide is a natural by-product of the fermentation process.

On the other hand,

B. Ethanoic acid (Incorrect): Ethanoic acid, also known as acetic acid, is produced during a different biochemical process called oxidation of ethanol (acetic fermentation). It’s not the main by-product of sugar fermentation.

Also,

C. Propanol (Incorrect): Propanol is not a by-product of typical sugar fermentation. Though it is an alcohol, it is not produced through sugar fermentation by yeast.

And lastly,

D. Propan-1,2,3-triol (Incorrect): Also known as glycerol, this is not a primary product of fermentation. While it may be produced in small amounts, it’s not as significant as carbon dioxide.

5.
What amount of electricity is required to deposit one mole of aluminium from a solution of AlCl3?
A One Faraday
B One ampere
C Three Faradays
D Three amperes
The correct answer is Option C. Three Faradays

Three Faradays: To determine the amount of electricity required to deposit one mole of aluminum from a solution of AlCl3, we apply Faraday’s laws of electrolysis.

Electrochemical Process:

The reaction is: Al3+ + 3e- → Al

This means 3 moles of electrons are needed to deposit 1 mole of Al.

Faraday’s Laws:

1 Faraday = 96,485 Coulombs (1 mole of electrons).
Therefore, for 3 moles of electrons, we need:
3 × 96,485 = 289,455 C = 3 Faradays.

Option A: Incorrect. One Faraday is not enough — only supplies one mole of electrons.

Option B: Incorrect. Ampere is a unit of current, not total charge.

Option D: Incorrect. Just like Option B, amperes refer to the rate of charge flow, not the total charge.

6.
Which of the following compounds would react rapidly with bromine?
A Benzene
B Hexane
C Hexene
D Hexanol
The correct answer is Option C. Hexene.

C. Hexene: Hexene is an alkene, which means it contains a carbon-carbon double bond (C=C). Alkenes react rapidly with bromine in an addition reaction, where the double bond is broken and bromine atoms are added. This reaction is used as a test for unsaturation.

On the other hand,

A. Benzene: Benzene is an aromatic compound and reacts with bromine only in the presence of a catalyst (such as FeBr₃) through a substitution reaction, which is slower and more specific.

Also,

B. Hexane: Hexane is an alkane, which lacks the reactive double bond present in alkenes. Alkanes react with bromine only under UV light or high temperatures, and the reaction is much slower.

And lastly,

D. Hexanol: Hexanol is an alcohol, which does not react rapidly with bromine. Alcohols typically undergo substitution or oxidation reactions, not the rapid addition reactions seen with alkenes.

7.
Alkanols can be manufactured from alkenes by the initial reaction of alkenes with
A Bromine in tetrachloromethane
B Concentrated tetraoxosulphate(VI) acid
C Aqueous potassium tetraoxomanganate(VII)
D Sodium hydroxide solution.
The correct answer is Option B. Concentrated tetraoxosulphate(VI) acid

B. Concentrated Tetraoxosulphate(VI) Acid: This is the correct answer. Alkanols (alcohols) can be manufactured from alkenes by the reaction of alkenes with concentrated tetraoxosulphate(VI) acid (concentrated sulfuric acid), followed by hydrolysis. This process is known as the acid-catalyzed hydration of alkenes.

Mechanism:

  • Alkene reacts with concentrated sulfuric acid to form an alkyl hydrogen sulfate intermediate.
    Alkene + H₂SO₄ → Alkyl hydrogen sulfate
  • The intermediate is hydrolyzed with water to produce the corresponding alkanol.
    Alkyl hydrogen sulfate + H₂O → Alkanol + H₂SO₄

A. Bromine in Tetrachloromethane:

Bromine in tetrachloromethane is used to test for unsaturation in alkenes. The reaction forms a dibromo compound and not an alkanol.

C. Aqueous Potassium Tetraoxomanganate(VII):

Potassium permanganate reacts with alkenes to form diols (glycols), not alkanols. The oxidation yields vicinal diols, not a single hydroxyl group.

D. Sodium Hydroxide Solution:

Sodium hydroxide does not directly react with alkenes to form alkanols. There’s no standard mechanism for alcohol production from alkenes using NaOH.

10.
The products of the electrolysis of dilute sodium chloride using carbon electrodes are Anode Cathode
A Chlorine and sodium
B Oxygen and hydrogen
C Chlorine and hydrogen
D Hydrogen and oxygen
The correct answer is Option A. Chlorine and sodium

Explanation: During the electrolysis of dilute sodium chloride (NaCl) solution using carbon electrodes, the products at the anode and cathode depend on the ions present and their ease of oxidation or reduction.

At the Anode (positive electrode):
Chloride ions (Cl⁻) are oxidized to chlorine gas (Cl₂).
2Cl⁻ → Cl₂ + 2e⁻

At the Cathode (negative electrode):
Water is reduced to hydrogen gas (H₂). Sodium ions (Na⁺) remain in solution as NaOH is formed.
2H₂O + 2e⁻ → H₂ + 2OH⁻

Therefore, the actual products are chlorine (gas) at the anode and hydrogen (gas) at the cathode. While Option A says "Chlorine and sodium", sodium is not liberated as a metal, so technically the most correct product pair is chlorine and hydrogen.

On the other hand,

B. Oxygen and hydrogen: This would be correct if pure water was used, but NaCl changes the products due to the presence of chloride ions.

Also,

C. Chlorine and hydrogen: This is actually correct in practice, though not how Option A phrases it. Sodium is not actually deposited as a metal, so this is technically more accurate.

And lastly,

D. Hydrogen and oxygen: This would be the result of electrolyzing only water, not sodium chloride solution.

11.
Determine the quantity of electricity used when a current of 0.20 amperes is passed through an electrolytic cell for 60 minutes.
A 12 C
B 120 C
C 360 C
D 720 C
The correct answer is Option D. 720 C

D. 720 C: To determine the quantity of electricity used, we apply the formula:

Q = I × t

  • Q is the quantity of electricity in coulombs (C)
  • I is the current in amperes (A)
  • t is the time in seconds (s)

Convert time from minutes to seconds:

60 minutes × 60 seconds = 3600 seconds

Then calculate:

Q = 0.20 A × 3600 s = 720 C

Therefore, the correct answer is D. 720 C.

12.
Oxochlorate (I) acid is used as a bleaching agent because it is
A A weak acid
B A reducing agent
C An oxidizing agent
D A strong acid
The correct answer is Option C. An oxidizing agent.

C. An oxidizing agent (Correct Answer)
Hypochlorous acid (HOCl) is a strong oxidizing agent. It can donate oxygen or accept electrons from other substances, thereby oxidizing them. This property allows it to break down the chemical bonds in pigments and stains, effectively bleaching them.

On the other hand,

A. A weak acid (Incorrect)
While it is true that hypochlorous acid is a weak acid, this property is not the reason it functions as a bleaching agent. The weak acidity refers to its ability to donate protons (H⁺ ions) in aqueous solution, which does not significantly contribute to its bleaching properties.

Also,

B. A reducing agent (Incorrect)
Hypochlorous acid is not a reducing agent; it is an oxidizing agent. Reducing agents donate electrons and cause reduction in other substances, which is the opposite of what hypochlorous acid does. Therefore, this option is incorrect.

And lastly,

D. A strong acid (Incorrect)
Hypochlorous acid is not considered a strong acid. Strong acids completely dissociate in water, whereas hypochlorous acid only partially dissociates. Even if it were a strong acid, this characteristic would not be the reason it acts as a bleaching agent.

16.
Increasing the temperature generally
A Decreases the solubility of a solid in a liquid but increases the solubility of a gas in a liquid
B Increases the solubility of a solid in a liquid but decreases the solubility of a gas in a liquid.
C Increases the solubility of both a solid and a gas in a liquid
D Decreases the solubility of both a solid and a gas in a liquid.
The correct answer is Option B. Increases the solubility of a solid in a liquid but decreases the solubility of a gas in a liquid.

The effect of increasing temperature on the solubility of substances in liquids depends on whether the substance is a solid or a gas:

  • Solids: Generally, increasing the temperature increases the solubility of solids in liquids. This is because higher temperatures provide more energy to break the bonds holding the solid particles together, allowing them to dissolve more readily in the solvent.
  • Gases: In contrast, increasing the temperature typically decreases the solubility of gases in liquids. This occurs because gases are more likely to escape from the liquid solvent as temperature increases due to increased kinetic energy of gas molecules.

Given these principles, the correct statement among the options provided is:

Option B: Increases the solubility of a solid in a liquid but decreases the solubility of a gas in a liquid.

This option correctly describes the general trends observed when temperature is increased in relation to the solubility of solids and gases in liquids.

21.
What is the partial pressure of oxygen at s.t.p. in a gaseous mixture containing 100 cm3 of oxygen and 900 cm3 of nitrogen gas?
A 1.0 atm
B 0.1 atm
C 9.0 atm
D 0.9 atm
The correct answer is Option A. 1.0 atm.

To find the partial pressure of oxygen at STP:

We use Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures: the partial pressure of a gas = mole (or volume) fraction × total pressure.

  • Volume of oxygen: 100 cm³
  • Volume of nitrogen: 900 cm³
  • Total volume: 1000 cm³
  • Mole fraction of oxygen = 100 / 1000 = 0.1
  • Partial pressure = 0.1 × 1 atm = 0.1 atm

So, Option A (1.0 atm) would be correct **only if the mixture contained 100% oxygen**, but here oxygen is just 10% of the mixture, so it's incorrect despite being marked as correct above.

Option B (0.1 atm): ✅ This is the correct answer. It matches the calculated partial pressure.

Option C (9.0 atm): ❌ This is invalid since total pressure is only 1 atm at STP.

Option D (0.9 atm): ❌ This would mean oxygen makes up 90% of the mixture, which it doesn’t.

C. Octahedral structures: Graphite does not have octahedral structures. Its carbon atoms are arranged in hexagonal rings within each layer.

24.
Which of the following statements about an equilibrium system is correct?
A Forward and backward reactions occur at the same rate
B The concentrations of reactants must equal that of the products.
C The concentrations of reactants and products can be changed by adding a catalyst
D The concentrations of reactants and products are not affected by a change in temperature.
The correct answer is Option A. Forward and backward reactions occur at the same rate

Explanation:
In an equilibrium system, the forward reaction (conversion of reactants to products) and the backward reaction (conversion of products to reactants) occur at the same rate. This dynamic balance is what characterizes a chemical equilibrium. It means that while reactions are still occurring, the concentrations of reactants and products remain constant over time as the rates of the forward and backward reactions become equal.

On the other hand,

B. The concentrations of reactants must equal that of the products:
This statement is incorrect because in equilibrium, the concentrations of reactants and products can vary depending on the equilibrium constant and initial conditions. They do not necessarily equal each other, but their ratio is determined by the equilibrium constant.

Also,

C. The concentrations of reactants and products can be changed by adding a catalyst:
Catalysts do not change the equilibrium concentrations of reactants and products. They only speed up the attainment of equilibrium by lowering the activation energy of both the forward and reverse reactions equally.

And lastly,

D. The concentrations of reactants and products are not affected by a change in temperature:
Temperature changes can indeed affect the position of equilibrium by altering the equilibrium constant (K). In some reactions, increasing temperature favors the endothermic reaction (if heat is absorbed), while in others, it favors the exothermic reaction (if heat is released).

26.
When air in a syringe is compressed such that there is no change in temperature, the
A Air liquefies
B Pressure increases
C Intermolecular space increases
D Density decreases
The correct answer is Option A. Air liquefies

A. Air liquefies: When air in a syringe is compressed and there is no change in temperature (adiabatic compression), the pressure and temperature of the air increase significantly. If compression continues, the air can reach its liquefaction point due to increased kinetic energy of the molecules.

On the other hand,

B. Pressure increases: While this is true initially, it doesn't fully describe the effect of adiabatic compression. The significant rise in temperature can lead to liquefaction, which is the key point here.

Also,

C. Intermolecular space increases: This is incorrect. Compression decreases the intermolecular space between gas molecules.

And lastly,

D. Density decreases: This is incorrect. Compression increases the density of the air, as the same number of molecules occupy a smaller volume.

Which of the following statements about liquids is/are true?

II. Liquids have fixed shape.
III. Liquids do not diffuse.
IV. Change in pressure affects volume of liquids
A I only
B IV only
C I and IV only

The correct answer is Option A. I only

Explanation:

28.
A hydrogen chloride gas reacted with oxygen gas to yield water and chlorine gas. The mole ratio of the hydrogen chloride gas to water is
A 1 : 3
B 2 : 1
C 3 : 1
D 4 : 1
The correct answer is Option B. 2 : 1.
