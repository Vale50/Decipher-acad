1.

The by-product of fermentation of sugar is

A. carbon(IV) oxide
B. ethanoic acid
C. propanol.
D. propan-1, 2, 3-triol.
The correct answer is A. carbon(IV) oxide During fermentation, microorganisms such as yeast break down sugars (such as glucose) into simpler substances, primarily ethanol (ethyl alcohol) and carbon dioxide gas (carbon(IV) oxide).
This process is anaerobic, meaning it occurs in the absence of oxygen. The equation for fermentation of glucose by yeast can be represented as: C6H12O6 → 2C2H5OH + 2CO2 Ethanoic acid (option B) is produced through a different process called aerobic respiration, which occurs in the presence of oxygen and involves the complete breakdown of glucose molecules. Propanol (option C) and propan-1, 2, 3-triol (option D) are not typically produced as by-products of sugar fermentation. Propanol may be produced through other chemical processes, and propan-1, 2, 3-triol is commonly known as glycerol, which is not a typical by-product of sugar fermentation.

7.

Alkanols can be manufactured from alkenes by the initial reaction of alkenes with

A. bromine in tetrachloromethane
B. concentrated tetraoxosulphate(VI) acid
C. aqueous potassium tetraoxomanganate(VII)
D. sodium hydroxide solution.
The correct answer is B. concentrated tetraoxosulphate(VI) acid. The reason for this is that the reaction between alkenes and sodium hydroxide is known as the hydrolysis reaction or alkaline hydrolysis. During this reaction, the alkene reacts with sodium hydroxide in the presence of water to produce alkanol (also known as alcohol) as the main product1. This process is a type of addition reaction where the double bond of the alkene opens up to bond with the hydroxyl group (-OH) from the water, forming an alcohol. The other options (A, B, and C) do not result in the formation of alkanols through this specific reaction mechanism.

If 5.0 g of marble reacts with 25.0 cm3 hydrochloric acid, which of the following combinations has the fastest reaction rate?

A. Marble chips and 2.0 mol dm-3 HCl(aq)
B. Marble chips and 2.5 mol dm-3 HCl(aq)
C. Powdered marble and 2.5 mol dmr-3 HC1(aq)
D. Powdered marble and 2.0 mol dm-3 HC1(aq)
The correct answer is C. Powdered marble and 2.5 mol dmr-3 HC1(aq) This is because the reaction rate is influenced by both the surface area of the marble and the concentration of hydrochloric acid (HCl). Surface Area: Powdered marble has a greater surface area compared to marble chips, allowing more acid to come into contact with the marble at the same time, thus increasing the rate of reaction. Concentration: A higher concentration of acid means more reactant particles per unit volume, leading to a higher probability of collisions between reactant particles, which increases the rate of reaction. Combining these two factors, powdered marble with a higher concentration of HCl (2.5 mol dm^-3) will have the fastest reaction rate.
