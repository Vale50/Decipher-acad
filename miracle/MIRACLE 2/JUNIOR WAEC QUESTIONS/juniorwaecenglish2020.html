14.

Obi and Chioma went hunting today.________

A. Isn't it?
B. Didn't they?
C.Hadn't they?
D. Haven't they?
The correct answer is Option B. Didn't they?.



When forming a question tag (a short question attached to a statement to confirm or seek agreement), it should typically match the tense and auxiliary verb used in the statement itself. In the statement "Obi and Chioma went hunting today," the main verb is "went," which is in the past simple tense. To form the question tag, we typically use the auxiliary verb that corresponds to the tense of the main verb. In this case, "went" is past tense, and the corresponding question tag auxiliary for past simple tense is "didn't" (short for "did not"). Therefore, "didn't they?" is the correct question tag to use because it matches the past tense of the statement.

On the other hand,
**A. Isn't it?**: This question tag is used for statements in the present tense, not the past tense as in the given statement.

Also,
**C. Hadn't they?**: This form suggests a past perfect tense, which is not appropriate here because the statement uses simple past tense ("went").

And lastly,
**D. Haven't they?**: This form uses present perfect tense, which again does not match the past tense used in the statement.
