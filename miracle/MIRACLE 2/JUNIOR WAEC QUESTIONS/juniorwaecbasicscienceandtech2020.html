Which of these gear is used to transmit motion between two parallel shafts.

A. Bevel gear
B. Helical gear
C. Spur gear
D. Worn gear
The correct answer is Options C. Spur gear.



**Helical Gear:** - Helical gears are used to transmit motion between two parallel shafts. Their teeth are cut at an angle to the face of the gear, allowing for gradual engagement and smoother, quieter operation compared to spur gears. The angle of the teeth also allows for more contact between the gears, increasing their load-carrying capacity.

On the other hand,
**Bevel Gear:** - Bevel gears are used to transmit motion between intersecting shafts, typically at a 90-degree angle. They are not suitable for parallel shafts.

Also,
**Spur Gear:** - Spur gears can also transmit motion between parallel shafts, similar to helical gears. However, helical gears are generally preferred for this purpose due to their smoother and quieter operation.

And lastly,
**Worm Gear:** - Worm gears are used to transmit motion between non-parallel and non-intersecting shafts, usually at a right angle. They are not suitable for parallel shafts.

The sap of timber tree is transported to leaves through _______

A. Stem
B. Root
C. Medullary rays
D. Bark
The correct answer is Options A. Stem.



**Stem**: The stem contains the vascular tissues—xylem and phloem—which are responsible for the transport of water, nutrients, and sap. The xylem transports water and dissolved minerals from the roots to the leaves, while the phloem distributes the sugars produced by photosynthesis from the leaves to other parts of the plant.

On the other hand,
**Root**: The roots absorb water and minerals from the soil, which are then transported upwards through the xylem in the stem to reach the leaves. However, roots themselves do not directly transport sap to the leaves.

Also,
**Medullary rays**: Medullary rays are radial sheets or ribbons extending vertically through the tree from the center outwards. They transport nutrients laterally within the tree, but they are not the main pathway for sap transport to the leaves.

And lastly,
**Bark**: The bark is the outermost layer of the tree, protecting it from physical damage and disease. It does not play a direct role in transporting sap to the leaves. The inner part of the bark, called the phloem, is involved in transporting nutrients, but the main transport of water and sap to the leaves occurs through the xylem in the stem.
