Example:

INSTRUCTION: Choose from the options lettered A-D, the one that best answers each of the following questions

Which of these is a solid matter?

A. Aluminum
B. Alcohol
C. Kerosine
D. Petroleum
The correct answer is Option A. Aluminum



Aluminum is a metal that remains solid under normal conditions, including room temperature.

On the other hand,

Alcohol is a liquid at room temperature. It does not have a fixed shape but takes the shape of its container, which is a characteristic of liquids, not solids.

Also,
Kerosene is a type of hydrocarbon that is commonly used as a fuel. At room temperature and pressure, kerosene is a liquid, not a solid.
And lastly,
Petroleum is a naturally occurring liquid found in rock formations. It consists of a complex mixture of hydrocarbons. Like alcohol and kerosene, petroleum is a liquid at room temperature.
2.

The S.I unit of force is....

A. Ampere.
B. kilogram
C. Metres per second
D. Newton
The correct answer is Option D. Newton.



Newton is the International System of Units (S.I) unit of force. It is defined as the force necessary to accelerate a one-kilogram mass by one meter per second squared.

On the other hand,

Option A. Ampere is incorrect because an ampere, often shortened to “amp,” is the base unit of electric current in the International System of Units. It is not related to force.

Also,
Option B. Kilogram is also incorrect. A kilogram is the base unit of mass in the International System of Units. While mass is related to force (via Newton’s second law of motion), it is not a unit of force.
And lastly,
Option C. Metres per second is incorrect as well. Metres per second is a unit of speed, not force. It describes how fast an object is moving, but not the force acting on it.
