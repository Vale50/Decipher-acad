In each of the following sentences, there is one underlined word and one gap. From the list of words lettered A to D, choose the one that is most nearly opposite in meaning to the underlined word and that will at the same time, correctly fill the gap in the sentence.

While Mr. Ade is……, his son is a novice.

A. Enlightened
B. Competent
C. Clever
D. Experienced
The correct answer is Option D. Newton.



Newton is the International System of Units (S.I) unit of force. It is defined as the force necessary to accelerate a one-kilogram mass by one meter per second squared.

On the other hand,

Option A. Ampere is incorrect because an ampere, often shortened to “amp,” is the base unit of electric current in the International System of Units. It is not related to force.

Also,
Option B. Kilogram is also incorrect. A kilogram is the base unit of mass in the International System of Units. While mass is related to force (via Newton’s second law of motion), it is not a unit of force.
And lastly,
Option C. Metres per second is incorrect as well. Metres per second is a unit of speed, not force. It describes how fast an object is moving, but not the force acting on it.

9.

The champions were elated after the tournament while their opponents looked

A. Baffled
B. Dejected
C. Pitiful
D. Frightened
The correct answer is B. Dejected The sentence is describing the contrasting emotions of the champions and their opponents after a tournament. The champions are described as being ‘elated’, which means they are very happy or proud. The most appropriate contrasting emotion for the opponents would be ‘dejected’, which means they are sad and depressed, likely because they lost the tournament


A. Baffled: This implies the opponents are confused or perplexed, which is not the most fitting emotion in this context. C. Pitiful: This term is more of an external observation that can be made by others, rather than an emotion experienced by the opponents themselves. D. Frightened: This suggests fear, which is not a typical reaction after losing a tournament. It doesn’t contrast appropriately with ‘elated’.

10: That engineer was said to have used inferior rather than…… materials in the construction of the collapsed building. .

A. Excellent
B. Recommended
C. Quality
D. Exceptional
The correct answer is C. Quality materials refer to materials that meet certain standards of excellence and reliability, which is what one would expect to be used in construction. Using inferior rather than quality materials could lead to a building collapse.


A. Excellent: While this term does imply a high standard, it’s more subjective and less specific than “quality,” which has a more defined meaning in the context of construction materials. B. Recommended: This could vary depending on who is making the recommendation. It doesn’t necessarily mean the materials are of good quality. D. Exceptional: This term implies that the materials are extraordinary or superior in some way, which might not be necessary for standard construction. Quality materials, on the other hand, simply need to meet certain standards.
