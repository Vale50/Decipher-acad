The instrument used to measure relative humidity is a
A Barometer
B Hydrometer
C Hygrometer
D Manometer
The correct answer is Option C. Hygrometer.

C. Hygrometer: This is the correct instrument for measuring relative humidity. It measures the amount of water vapor in the air compared to the maximum amount the air can hold at that temperature.

On the other hand,

A. Barometer: This instrument is used to measure atmospheric pressure, not humidity. It helps in weather forecasting and determining altitude.

Also,

B. Hydrometer: This instrument is used to measure the density or specific gravity of liquids, not humidity. It's often used in brewing, winemaking, and battery testing.

And lastly,

D. Manometer: This instrument measures the pressure of gases or liquids. It is not used for measuring humidity but is often used in laboratories to measure gas pressures in closed systems.

B 2.93 x 1015 Hz

The correct answer is Option A. 1.24 x 1015 Hz.

To determine the frequency of the absorbed radiation when an electron moves from an energy level of -8.6 eV to another level of -3.5 eV, we need to calculate the energy difference and then use the relationship between energy and frequency.

Step 1: Calculate the energy difference (ΔE)
The energy difference between the two levels is given by:
ΔE = Efinal - Einitial
ΔE = (-3.5 eV) - (-8.6 eV) = 5.1 eV

Step 2: Convert the energy difference to joules (J)
ΔE = 5.1 eV × 1.6 × 10-19 J/eV = 8.16 × 10-19 J

Step 3: Use Planck's equation to find the frequency (f)
E = hf, therefore f = E/h
f = (8.16 × 10-19 J) / (6.6 × 10-34 Js) = 1.24 × 1015 Hz

The equation is:
v² = u² + 2gh
Where: v = final velocity (5.0 m/s), u = initial velocity (0 m/s), g = acceleration due to gravity (10 m/s²), h = height

Solving:
(5.0)² = (0)² + 2 × 10 × h
25 = 20h
h = 25/20 = 1.25 m

On the other hand,

B. 1.60 m: This value is incorrect because it does not satisfy the kinematic equation with the given values for final velocity and gravitational acceleration.

Also,

C. 4.00 m: This height would result in a much higher final velocity if dropped from rest, not 5.0 m/s.

And lastly,

D. 6.25 m: This is also incorrect for similar reasons, as it would imply a higher final velocity than 5.0 m/s if dropped from that height.

Latent heat refers to the heat absorbed or released by a substance during a phase change without changing its temperature. Here's a breakdown of each statement:

Increasing the frequency of a sound wave produces a sound with
A Longer wavelength
B Reduced loudness
C Higher pitch
D More overtones
The correct answer is Option C. Higher pitch.

C. Higher pitch: This is correct. Pitch is the perceived frequency of a sound. As frequency increases, the pitch of the sound becomes higher. This is why a higher frequency sound, such as that from a piccolo, is perceived as higher in pitch compared to a lower frequency sound, such as that from a bass drum.

On the other hand,

A. Longer wavelength: This is incorrect. The wavelength of a wave is inversely proportional to its frequency. Increasing the frequency actually decreases the wavelength.

Also,

B. Reduced loudness: This is incorrect. The loudness of a sound is determined by its amplitude, not its frequency. Increasing frequency doesn't necessarily affect loudness.

And lastly,

D. More overtones: This is incorrect. Overtones are higher frequency vibrations that occur along with the fundamental frequency of a sound. The presence and number of overtones are more related to the nature of the sound source rather than simply increasing the frequency of a sound wave.

Therefore, inserting a brass rod into the solenoid will not increase the strength of the magnetic field. In conclusion, the correct answer (Option B) is the brass rod because it is non-magnetic and does not increase the magnetic field strength when inserted into the solenoid. Options A, C, and D involve ferromagnetic materials with high permeability, which effectively increase the magnetic field strength when inserted into the solenoid.

On the other hand,

A. Nickel rod in the test tube: Nickel is a ferromagnetic material with higher permeability than air or vacuum. When inserted into the solenoid, it increases the magnetic field strength because it concentrates the magnetic flux lines and enhances the overall magnetic field.

Also,

C. Soft iron rod in the test tube: Soft iron is also a ferromagnetic material with very high permeability. It is easily magnetized and demagnetized, making it highly effective at concentrating magnetic fields. And lastly,

D. Steel rod in the test tube: Steel, like iron, is a ferromagnetic material with high permeability. It behaves similarly to soft iron but with slightly different magnetic properties. C. The capacitance of the capacitor for given dimensions is much greater than that of the plates in a vacuum: This is also true. Using a solid dielectric material increases the capacitance of the capacitor for a given physical size compared to a vacuum or air. 3. Frictional Force:
F_friction = μN = 0.25 × 866 = 216.5 N

4. Total Opposing Force:
F_opposing = F∥ + F_friction = 500 N + 216.5 N = 716.5 N

On the other hand,

C Six times as far
D At the same distance as A
The correct answer is Option A. Two times as far.

Therefore s_B = 2s_A, meaning B travels twice as far as A.

On the other hand,

B. Three times as far: This is incorrect. The relationship between acceleration and distance for objects starting from rest follows s ∝ a, so doubling acceleration results in doubling the distance, not tripling it.

Also,

C. Six times as far: This is also incorrect. This would imply a much different relationship between acceleration and distance that doesn't exist in uniform acceleration scenarios.

And lastly,

D. At the same distance as A: This is incorrect. Due to its higher acceleration rate, B will cover a greater distance than A over the same time period.
