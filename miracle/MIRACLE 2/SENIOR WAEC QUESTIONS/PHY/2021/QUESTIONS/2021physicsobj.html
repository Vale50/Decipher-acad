1.
Which of the following quantities is not an example of force?
A Mass
B Tension
C Weight
D Friction
The correct answer is Option A. Mass

A. Mass: Mass is a measure of the amount of matter in an object, typically measured in kilograms (kg). It is a scalar quantity and does not have a direction. Mass is not a force; rather, it measures the inertia of an object, or how much it resists acceleration when a force is applied.

B. Tension: Tension is a force transmitted through strings, ropes, cables, etc., when pulled tight from opposite ends. It is a vector quantity, having both magnitude and direction.

C. Weight: Weight is the force exerted on a mass by gravity. It is calculated as the mass of an object multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity (Weight = Mass × g).

D. Friction: Friction is the force that opposes the relative motion or tendency of such motion between two surfaces in contact. It acts parallel to the surfaces and is a vector quantity.

Summary: Mass is the only quantity listed that is not a force, making it the correct answer.

2.
The reason for laminating the soft iron core of a transformer is to _____
A Reduce eddy current
B Reduce the magnetic flux
C Increase the magnetic flux
D Reduce its efficiency
The correct answer is Option A. Reduce eddy current. The reason for laminating the soft iron core of a transformer is to reduce eddy current.

A. Reduce eddy current: Laminating the soft iron core helps to reduce the formation of eddy currents. Eddy currents are loops of electric current induced within the core due to the changing magnetic field. These currents can generate significant heat and result in energy losses. By laminating the core, these currents are minimized, improving the efficiency of the transformer.

On the other hand,

B. Reduce the magnetic flux: Incorrect. Laminations don't reduce magnetic flux. In fact, the core is meant to efficiently guide it.

Also,

C. Increase the magnetic flux: While the material helps increase flux, lamination specifically reduces eddy currents.

And lastly,

D. Reduce its efficiency: Incorrect. Laminating the core actually improves efficiency by reducing heat losses.

3.
Formation of hydrogen bubbles at the copper plate of a primary cell is called
A Polarization
B Back e.m.f.
C Electrolysis
D Local action
The correct answer is Option A. Polarization. The formation of hydrogen bubbles at the copper plate of a primary cell is known as polarization.

A. Polarization: This refers to the build-up of hydrogen gas bubbles on the cathode in a primary cell, increasing internal resistance and reducing efficiency.

On the other hand,

B. Back e.m.f.: This occurs in motors/generators and is unrelated to hydrogen formation in primary cells.

Also,

C. Electrolysis: Involves externally applied voltage to split substances—not the same as polarization in a cell.

And lastly,

D. Local Action: Refers to self-discharge caused by impurities in electrodes—not hydrogen bubble formation.

Conclusion: The correct term for hydrogen build-up at the copper plate is polarization, which hinders current flow and increases internal resistance.

The correct answer is Option D. Exhibit vibratory motion.

On the other hand,

9.
The distance between a node and its adjacent antinode of a transverse wave is equal to
A Half of the wavelength
B The wavelength
C Thrice the wavelength
D Quarter of the wavelength
The correct answer is Option D. Quarter of the wavelength.

D. Quarter of the wavelength: In a standing wave, the distance between a node and the nearest antinode is one-quarter of the wavelength (λ/4). This is because the wave cycle is divided into four parts: node → antinode → node → antinode.

On the other hand,

A. Half of the wavelength: This is incorrect. Half of the wavelength (λ/2) is the distance between two successive nodes or two successive antinodes.

Also,

B. The wavelength: This is incorrect because one full wavelength is the distance between two identical points in a cycle, such as two antinodes. The node-to-antinode distance is much shorter.

And lastly,

C. Thrice the wavelength: This is incorrect. 3λ doesn't represent the distance between a node and adjacent antinode in any standard standing wave relationship.

11.
Which of the following actions will increase the capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor?
I. Decreasing the distance between the plates
II. Increasing the distance between the plates
III. Increasing the area of the plates overlap
IV. Avoiding the use of dielectric between the plates
A I and III only
B II and III only
C I and IV only
D III and IV only
The correct answer is Option A. I and III only

I. Decreasing the distance between the plates:
Capacitance \( C \) of a parallel plate capacitor is given by:
\( C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 \cdot A}{d} \)
where \( \varepsilon_0 \) is the permittivity of free space, \( A \) is the plate area, and \( d \) is the plate distance.
So, reducing \( d \) increases \( C \).

III. Increasing the area of plate overlap:
Since \( C \propto A \), increasing the area \( A \) also increases the capacitance.

II. Increasing the distance between the plates:
This decreases capacitance, so it's incorrect.

IV. Avoiding the use of dielectric:
A dielectric increases capacitance. Avoiding it would decrease capacitance.

Therefore, only options I and III increase the capacitance.

15.
The handle of a screw jack is 35 cm long and the pitch of the screw is 0.5 cm. What force must be applied at the end of the handle to lift a load of 2000 N if the efficiency of the jack is 30%? [π = 22/7]
A 152.0 N
B 132.0 N
C 15.2 N
D 440.0 N
The correct answer is Option C. 15.2 N

C. 15.2 N: To solve this problem, we use principles of mechanics and efficiency of the jack.

  • Circumference of handle: 2πr = 2 × 22/7 × 35 = 220 cm
  • Pitch: 0.5 cm
  • Mechanical Advantage (MA): 220 / 0.5 = 440
  • Actual MA (considering 30% efficiency): 440 × 0.30 = 132
  • Force: 2000 ÷ 132 ≈ 15.2 N

A. 152.0 N: Too large – incorrect.

B. 132.0 N: This is the MA, not the force.

D. 440.0 N: This is the initial MA, not the required force.

19.
Which of the following units is equivalent to the unit of electric current?
A C s-1
B W
C J s-1
D C
The correct answer is Option A. C s-1.

Electric current (I) is defined as the flow of electric charge (Q) per unit time (t). Mathematically, I = Q / t.

The unit of electric charge (Q) is the coulomb (C), and the unit of time (t) is the second (s). Therefore, the unit of electric current is C s-1, which is equivalent to the ampere (A).

Option B: W (watt) – Incorrect. This is the unit of power, not electric current.

Option C: J s-1 – Incorrect. This is also the unit of power (joules per second).

Option D: C (coulomb) – Incorrect. Coulomb is the unit of charge, not current.

20.
A ball is swung in a horizontal circle of centre O with a constant speed. What is the direction of its acceleration?
A Away from O
B Along the circumference of the circle
C Toward O
D Along a tangent to the circle
The correct answer is Option C. Toward O.

The acceleration of the ball is directed towards the center of the circle (O). This is known as centripetal acceleration.

Even though the speed is constant, the direction of motion is constantly changing, which means there is acceleration. This inward acceleration keeps the ball moving in a circular path.

Option A: Away from O – Incorrect. Acceleration is directed toward the center.

Option B: Along the circumference – Incorrect. Acceleration is not tangent but radial.

Option D: Along a tangent – Incorrect. Velocity is tangent to the circle, but acceleration is inward.
