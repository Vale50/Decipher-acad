1.

The study of economics is important to every society because it

A. Enables individuals to satisfy all their wants.
B. Helps in the utilization of scarce resources.
C. Helps producers to know what to produce.
D. Restores equilibrium between producers and consumers.
The correct answer is Option B. Helps in the utilization of scarce resources.



The correct answer is: B. Helps in the utilization of scarce resources. Explanation: Economics is fundamentally concerned with how societies manage scarce resources to meet their needs and wants. It studies how resources like land, labor, and capital are used to produce goods and services and how these are distributed among individuals. Since resources are limited, economics focuses on maximizing their efficiency and effectiveness to address scarcity and make informed decisions.

On the other hand,
A. Enables individuals to satisfy all their wants: Economics acknowledges that resources are limited, so it does not promise that all wants can be satisfied. Instead, it deals with how to make the best use of limited resources to address needs and prioritize wants.

Also,
C. Helps producers to know what to produce: While economics does provide insights into demand and supply which can guide producers, it does not directly instruct what to produce. The decision on what to produce is influenced by market signals, consumer preferences, and other factors.

And lastly,
D. Restores equilibrium between producers and consumers: Economics studies market equilibrium, but it does not restore it. It analyzes how markets reach equilibrium and the conditions under which it can be disrupted or restored, rather than actively engaging in restoring equilibrium.

A. Dress.
B. Jewellery.
C. Handbag and jewellery.
D. Dress and jewellery.
The correct answer is Option D. Dress and jewellery.



To determine the opportunity cost of buying the pair of shoes, you need to understand what you have to give up in order to acquire them. In this scenario: Cost of Pair of Shoes: $10 Available Budget: $10 Given the budget of $10 and the cost of the shoes being $10, purchasing the shoes means you can't afford any other items. Here’s the breakdown: Dress: $20 Handbag: $7 Jewellery: $3 If you spend $10 on shoes, you lose the opportunity to buy the dress, which costs more than your entire budget, and also the handbag and jewellery, which combined cost $10 ($7 + $3). Therefore, you forego the ability to buy the dress and the jewellery.

On the other hand,
A. Dress: This option is incorrect because the dress is not affordable with the remaining budget after purchasing shoes. It’s not just the dress you miss out on but also the jewellery.

Also,
B. Jewellery: This option is incorrect because while jewellery is missed out on, it is not the only item given up; you also miss the dress.

And lastly,
C. Handbag and jewellery: This option is incorrect because you miss out on more than just the handbag and jewellery; you also miss the dress.

When a commodity market operates without government interference, commodities are distributed through

A. Government distribution agencies.
B. The operation of price mechanism.
C. A central planning committee
D. Retailers only
The correct answer is Option B. The operation of price mechanism.



When a commodity market operates without government interference, the distribution of commodities is determined by the price mechanism. This system allows supply and demand to interact freely, setting prices based on how much consumers are willing to pay and how much producers are willing to supply. The price mechanism helps allocate resources efficiently by signaling producers and consumers when to adjust their behaviors based on the scarcity or abundance of goods.

On the other hand,
A. Government distribution agencies: This option is incorrect because government distribution agencies are involved when there is government interference. In a free market without government involvement, commodities are not distributed by such agencies.

Also,
C. A central planning committee: This option is incorrect because a central planning committee is typically associated with a command economy or centrally planned economy, where the government or a central authority makes decisions about the allocation of resources. In a free market, there is no central authority planning the distribution of commodities.

And lastly,
D. Retailers only: While retailers play a role in the distribution of commodities, they are not the sole mechanism by which distribution occurs in a free market. The price mechanism encompasses the broader process through which supply and demand interact to determine prices and distribution, involving various market participants beyond just retailers.

And lastly,

An increase in rice harvest, all things being equal, may cause

A. Price to increase substantially.
B. Price to fall substantially
C. Demand to fall substantially
D. Farmer’s incomes to be more than doubled.
The correct answer is Option B. Price to fall substantially



An increase in rice harvest, all things being equal (i.e., assuming no changes in demand or other factors), means there is more rice available in the market. According to the law of supply and demand, when the supply of a product increases and demand remains constant, the price of the product tends to fall. Therefore, an increase in rice harvest is likely to cause the price of rice to fall substantially.

On the other hand,
A. Price to increase substantially: This option is incorrect because an increase in supply typically leads to a decrease in price, not an increase. When supply increases, the surplus of the product usually drives prices down.

Also,
C. Demand to fall substantially: This option is incorrect because the change in the quantity of rice harvested affects supply, not demand. An increase in supply doesn't directly impact demand; demand is influenced by other factors such as consumer preferences, income levels, etc.

And lastly,
D. Farmer’s incomes to be more than doubled: This option is incorrect because while an increase in harvest might lead to more revenue for farmers if prices stay the same, the fall in price due to increased supply could offset this potential gain. Therefore, it is unlikely that farmer's incomes would more than double solely based on an increase in harvest without considering other economic factors.

And lastly,
D. Competitive demand: Competitive demand refers to the situation where products or services compete against each other for consumer spending. This is not related to the demand for factors of production.

10.

The coefficient of price elasticity of supply of land is usually

A. One.
B. Greater than one.
C. Zero.
D. Less than one.
The correct answer is Option C. Zero.



Price Elasticity of Supply measures how much the quantity supplied of a good responds to a change in its price. It is calculated as the percentage change in quantity supplied divided by the percentage change in price. Land is a fixed resource; its total supply cannot be increased or decreased in response to changes in its price. Because the supply of land is perfectly inelastic, the quantity of land remains constant regardless of price changes. This results in a price elasticity of supply of zero.

On the other hand,
A. One: This implies unit elasticity, where the percentage change in quantity supplied is exactly equal to the percentage change in price. However, since the supply of land cannot change, this is incorrect.

Also,
B. Greater than One: This suggests that the supply is elastic, meaning the quantity supplied changes more than the change in price. This doesn't apply to land because its supply does not change at all.

And lastly,
D. Less than One: This implies inelastic supply, where the quantity supplied changes but less proportionately to price changes. While land supply is indeed inelastic, the specific elasticity is zero, not just less than one. Therefore, this option is also incorrect.

A leftward shift in the supply curve for a commodity indicates

A. An increase in quantity supplied
B. A decrease in supply.
C. A reduction in quantity supplied.
D. An increase in supply
The correct answer is Option B. A decrease in supply.



B. A decrease in supply (Correct): A leftward shift in the supply curve indicates that at every price level, the quantity supplied has decreased. This could be due to various factors such as increased production costs, a decrease in the number of producers, or new regulations that limit production. This is the correct interpretation of a leftward shift in the supply curve.

On the other hand,
A. An increase in quantity supplied: This would typically correspond to a movement along the supply curve to the right, not a shift of the curve itself. Also,
C. A reduction in quantity supplied: This answer is partly correct but misleading. A reduction in quantity supplied would occur along the supply curve due to a decrease in the price of the commodity, not a shift of the entire curve. The leftward shift represents a decrease in supply, not just a reduction in quantity at a single price point.

And lastly,
D. An increase in supply: This is the opposite of what a leftward shift represents. An increase in supply would be shown by a rightward shift of the supply curve, where more of the commodity is supplied at every price level.

14.

Governments can increase farmers’ incomes by

A. Fixing maximum prices.
B. Fixing minimum prices.
C. Encouraging them to produce surplus output.
D. Increasing taxes on inputs
The correct answer is Option C. Encouraging them to produce surplus output.



C. Encouraging them to produce surplus output (Correct): When farmers are encouraged to produce more than what is needed for immediate consumption (surplus output), they can sell the excess in the market. This increased supply can lead to more income for the farmers, especially if the market demand is strong. Additionally, producing surplus can allow farmers to export their products or store them for future sales when prices might be higher, thus stabilizing their income over time.

On the other hand,
A. Fixing maximum prices (Incorrect): Fixing maximum prices means setting a ceiling on the price that can be charged for goods. This is typically done to protect consumers, not producers. For farmers, this can be harmful because it limits the amount they can earn from their products, reducing their potential income.

Also,
B. Fixing minimum prices (Incorrect): Fixing minimum prices can help to ensure that farmers receive a baseline income for their products, but it doesn't directly lead to an increase in income. If the minimum price is set too high, it can lead to excess supply, which might go unsold, or it might distort the market, leading to inefficiencies and possible waste.

And lastly,
D. Increasing taxes on inputs (Incorrect): Inputs are the resources used by farmers to produce goods, such as seeds, fertilizer, equipment, etc. Increasing taxes on these inputs would raise the cost of production for farmers, which could reduce their profitability and income.
15.

When market supply increases, the equilibrium price

A. Rises and quantity falls.
B. Falls and quantity rises.
C. And quantity increase.
D. And quantity fall.
The correct answer is Option B. Falls and quantity rises.



When market supply increases, more goods or services are available in the market at each price level. Equilibrium price is the price at which the quantity of goods supplied is equal to the quantity of goods demanded. Why B is Correct: Price Falls: An increase in supply, assuming demand remains constant, will lead to a surplus of goods at the original equilibrium price. To eliminate this surplus, the price must fall until the surplus is eliminated. Quantity Rises: As the price falls, consumers are encouraged to buy more, leading to an increase in the quantity demanded. At the new equilibrium, more goods are sold at a lower price.

On the other hand,
A. rises and quantity falls: This scenario would occur if the supply decreased, not increased. An increase in supply typically does not lead to a rise in price or a decrease in quantity.

Also,
C. and quantity increase: While an increase in supply can lead to a higher quantity, it does not lead to an increase in price. The price usually falls when supply increases, not rises.

And lastly,
D. and quantity fall: This would happen if there were a decrease in demand or an increase in supply with an even larger decrease in demand. However, with an increase in supply alone, the price falls, and quantity usually rises, not falls.

The law of diminishing returns is applicable to the

A. Fixed inputs of production.
B. Variable factors of production
C. Plants and machinery of the firm
D. Equipment and other capital

The Law of Diminishing Returns states that as more units of a variable input (like labor) are added to a fixed input (like land or machinery), the additional output (marginal product) produced by each additional unit of the variable input eventually decreases. This principle assumes that at least one input is fixed, meaning it does not change as production increases. A. fixed inputs of production: This is the correct answer because the law of diminishing returns applies when one input (e.g., land, machinery) is fixed. As more variable inputs (like labor) are added, the efficiency of production decreases after a certain point.

On the other hand,
B. variable factors of production: This is incorrect because the law specifically refers to the situation where some inputs are fixed. If all inputs were variable, this law wouldn’t apply.

Also,
C. plants and machinery of the firm: While plants and machinery can be considered fixed inputs, this option is incorrect because it is too specific. The law applies generally to any fixed input, not just plants and machinery.

And lastly,
D. equipment and other capital: Similar to option C, this is incorrect because it focuses on specific types of fixed inputs. The law applies broadly to any fixed inputs, not just equipment and capital.

The relationship between the marginal revenue (MR) and the average revenue (AR) of a monopolist is that the marginal revenue curve

A. Is above the average revenue curve.
B. Slopes down to the right and is below the AR curve.
C. And the AR curve are downward sloping and are identical.
D. Is vertical while the average revenue curve is horizontal.
The correct answer is Option B. Slopes down to the right and is below the AR curve.



B. MR slopes down to the right and is below the AR curve (Correct): In a monopoly, the average revenue (AR) curve represents the demand curve, which is downward sloping because the monopolist must lower the price to sell more units. The marginal revenue (MR) is the additional revenue gained from selling one more unit. For a monopolist, MR is always less than AR because to sell an additional unit, the monopolist must reduce the price, which reduces the revenue gained from all previous units sold at the higher price. Thus, the MR curve lies below the AR curve and also slopes downward.

On the other hand,
A. MR is above the AR curve (Incorrect): This is incorrect because MR is always below AR for a monopolist due to the need to reduce prices to sell additional units.

Also,
C. MR and AR curves are downward sloping and identical (Incorrect): While both MR and AR curves are downward sloping, they are not identical. MR is below AR due to the price reduction required to sell additional units.

And lastly,
D. MR is vertical while the AR curve is horizontal (Incorrect): This is incorrect because neither the MR curve nor the AR curve is vertical or horizontal. The AR curve is downward sloping, and the MR curve is also downward sloping but lies below the AR curve. Vertical or horizontal curves would not represent the relationship between MR and AR in a monopoly.
