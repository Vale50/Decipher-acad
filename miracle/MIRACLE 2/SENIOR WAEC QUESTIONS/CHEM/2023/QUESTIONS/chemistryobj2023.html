More Information

Collision theory states that for a reaction to occur, reactant particles must collide with sufficient energy and proper orientation. Not all collisions result in a reaction; only those that have enough energy to overcome the activation energy barrier and have the correct orientation will result in a successful or effective collision. Thus, the rate of a chemical reaction depends on the number of these effective collisions per unit time. The more effective collisions there are, the faster the reaction will proceed. Therefore, the statement "Rate of reaction is proportional to the number of effective collisions" is correct according to the collision theory.
