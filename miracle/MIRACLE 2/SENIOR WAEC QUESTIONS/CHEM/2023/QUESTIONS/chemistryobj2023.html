1.
Which of the following laws or theory cannot be explained by the application of kinetic theory of gases?
A Dalton’s atomic theory
B Charles’ law
C Gay-lussac’s law
D Boyle’s law
The correct answer is Option A. Dalton’s atomic theory.

Dalton’s atomic theory: This theory explains the nature of atoms and how they combine to form compounds. It includes postulates such as atoms being indivisible, identical for a given element, and that chemical reactions involve rearrangement of atoms. These are outside the scope of the kinetic theory of gases.

On the other hand,

Charles’ Law: States that at constant pressure, gas volume is directly proportional to temperature. The kinetic theory supports this by showing that higher temperatures increase molecular motion and therefore volume.

Also,

Gay-Lussac’s Law: States that at constant volume, pressure is directly proportional to temperature. The kinetic theory supports this by showing more forceful collisions at higher temperatures.

And lastly,

Boyle’s Law: States that at constant temperature, volume is inversely proportional to pressure. According to kinetic theory, reducing volume increases collision frequency, thus increasing pressure.

The correct answer is Option A. Involves decomposition.

A. Involves decomposition: Thermal cracking breaks down large hydrocarbon molecules (alkanes) into smaller molecules, including both alkanes and alkenes, through the application of high temperatures. This decomposition helps convert heavy fractions of petroleum into more useful products like gasoline and olefins.

On the other hand,

B. Is an exothermic process: Thermal cracking is an endothermic process, meaning it requires the input of heat to break down larger molecules into smaller ones.

Also,

C. Produces only small alkanes: Thermal cracking produces a mixture of smaller alkanes and alkenes, not exclusively small alkanes.

And lastly,

D. Requires hydrogen: Hydrogen is not required in thermal cracking; it is needed in hydrocracking, which is a different process.

C. Presence of additives in petrol: Additives can influence combustion efficiency and emissions, but they are not the primary reason for carbon deposits.

A 2
B 1
C 0.5
D 0.25
The correct answer is Option C. 0.5

To determine how many moles of copper are deposited by passing 1 Faraday of electricity through a CuCl2 solution, we apply Faraday's laws of electrolysis.

The half-reaction at the cathode is:
Cu2+ + 2e- → Cu

1 Faraday = 1 mole of electrons.
According to the reaction, 2 moles of electrons are needed to deposit 1 mole of copper.
So, passing 1 Faraday (1 mole of electrons) will deposit 0.5 moles of copper.

Therefore, the correct answer is: Option C. 0.5

If 100 cm3 of a saturated solution of sodium tetraoxosulphate (VI) at 30 °C contains 10.5 g of the salt, what would be its solubility at this temperature? [ Na2SO4 = 142 ]
A 0.57 mol dm-3
B 0.60 mol dm-3
C 0.74 mol dm-3
D 2.15 mol dm-3
The correct answer is Option C. 0.74 mol dm-3.

To determine the solubility of sodium tetraoxosulphate (VI) (Na2SO4) at 30°C, we start by calculating the number of moles of Na2SO4 dissolved in 100 cm³ (which is equivalent to 0.1 dm³) of saturated solution.

Given:
Mass of Na2SO4 = 10.5 g
Volume of solution = 100 cm³ = 0.1 dm³
Molar mass of Na2SO4 = 142 g/mol

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles
Number of moles = Mass / Molar mass = 10.5 / 142 = 0.07394 mol

Step 2: Calculate the solubility
Solubility = Moles / Volume = 0.07394 / 0.1 = 0.7394 mol dm-3
Rounded: 0.74 mol dm-3

Therefore, the solubility of sodium tetraoxosulphate (VI) at 30°C is 0.74 mol dm-3.

Sodium acetate dissociates in water to form sodium ions (Na⁺) and acetate ions (CH₃COO⁻). The acetate ion is the conjugate base of a weak acid (acetic acid) and reacts with water to form hydroxide ions (OH⁻), making the solution basic.

16.
Which of the following statements about the collision theory is correct?
A All collisions bring about reactions.
B Rate of reaction is proportional to the number of effective collisions
C
D
The correct answer is Option B. Rate of reaction is proportional to the number of effective collisions.

Collision theory states that for a reaction to occur, reactant particles must collide with sufficient energy and proper orientation. Not all collisions result in a reaction; only those that have enough energy to overcome the activation energy barrier and have the correct orientation will result in a successful or effective collision.

Thus, the rate of a chemical reaction depends on the number of these effective collisions per unit time. The more effective collisions there are, the faster the reaction will proceed.

Therefore, the statement "Rate of reaction is proportional to the number of effective collisions" is correct according to the collision theory.

How many unpaired electrons are present in 26Fe3+?

B 3

To determine the number of unpaired electrons in Fe3+, consider the electron configuration of iron (Fe):

  • Neutral Fe: Atomic number = 26 → Electron configuration: [Ar] 3d6 4s2
  • Fe3+: Loses two 4s electrons and one 3d electron → [Ar] 3d5

On the other hand:

A. 2: This is incorrect because it would imply electron pairing within the 3d orbitals, which contradicts Hund’s rule.

B. 3: Incorrect for the same reason — 3 unpaired electrons would not correctly represent the 3d5 configuration.

C. 4: Also incorrect — with 3d5, each of the 5 d orbitals has one electron each, all unpaired.

20.
Going down group II in the periodic table normally
A shielding effect decreases.
B melting point increases
C ionization energy decreases.
D electronegativity increases.
The correct answer is Option C. ionization energy decreases.

Ionization Energy: The ionization energy decreases as you go down the group. This is because the outermost electrons are further from the nucleus and are more shielded by the inner electrons, making them easier to remove.

On the other hand,

Shielding Effect: The shielding effect actually increases as you go down the group because each successive element has an additional electron shell, increasing the number of inner electrons that shield the outer electrons from the nucleus.

Also,

Melting Point: The melting points of Group II elements do not show a simple trend. While beryllium and magnesium have relatively high melting points, calcium, strontium, and barium have lower melting points. Thus, melting points generally decrease after magnesium.

And lastly,

Electronegativity: The electronegativity decreases down the group. Larger atoms with more electron shells have their outer electrons further from the nucleus, which makes them less attracted to electrons in a chemical bond.

The periodic property that is used to determine whether a covalent molecule is polar or not is
A atomic radius
B electron affinity
C electronegativity
D ionization energy
The correct answer is Option C. electronegativity.

C. electronegativity: The periodic property that is used to determine whether a covalent molecule is polar or not is electronegativity. Electronegativity is the tendency of an atom to attract a shared pair of electrons towards itself in a covalent bond. If two atoms in a covalent bond have different electronegativities, the shared electrons will be pulled closer to the more electronegative atom, resulting in a polar covalent bond. Therefore, electronegativity differences between atoms in a molecule help determine whether the molecule is polar or nonpolar.

On the other hand,

A. atomic radius: Atomic radius is not directly related to polarity determination.

Also,

B. electron affinity: Electron affinity refers to the energy released or absorbed when an electron is added to an atom, which is not directly used to determine polarity.

And lastly,

D. ionization energy: Ionization energy is the energy required to remove an electron from an atom, which is also not directly used to determine polarity.

Therefore, the relative atomic mass of element X is 65.40, which matches Option B.

28.
Which of the following gases has the lowest rate of diffusion?
[ H = 1.0, C = 12.0, N = 14.0, O = 16.0 ]
A Nitrogen
B Ammonia
C Oxygen
D Methane
The correct answer is Option C. Oxygen.

The rate of diffusion of a gas is inversely proportional to the square root of its molar mass, according to Graham's law of diffusion:

Rate of diffusion ∝ 1 / √(Molar mass)

Let's calculate the molar masses of each gas:

  • Nitrogen (N₂): 2 × 14 = 28 g/mol
  • Ammonia (NH₃): 14 + (3 × 1) = 17 g/mol
  • Oxygen (O₂): 2 × 16 = 32 g/mol
  • Methane (CH₄): 12 + (4 × 1) = 16 g/mol

Since diffusion is slower for gases with higher molar mass, Oxygen (32 g/mol) has the lowest rate of diffusion among the options provided.

Therefore, the correct answer is C. Oxygen.
