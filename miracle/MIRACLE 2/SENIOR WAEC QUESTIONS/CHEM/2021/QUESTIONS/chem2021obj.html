: A diatomic molecule is made up of two atoms. Examples include oxygen (\( \text{O}_2 \)) and nitrogen (\( \text{N}_2 \)). Phosphorus does not naturally exist as a diatomic molecule, so this option is incorrect.

: A triatomic molecule consists of three atoms. Examples include ozone (\( \text{O}_3 \)). Phosphorus does not form a stable triatomic molecule under normal conditions, so this option is incorrect.

: A monoatomic molecule consists of a single atom, such as the noble gases (e.g., helium (\( \text{He} \)), neon (\( \text{Ne} \))). Phosphorus does not exist as a single atom in its natural state, so this option is incorrect.
