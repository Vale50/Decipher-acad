1.
The hydrolysis of proteins by dilute mineral acids produces
A sucrose
B glucose
C amino acid
D fatty acids
The correct answer is Option C. amino acid.

1. Hydrolysis of Proteins:
- Proteins are long chains of amino acids linked together by peptide bonds. When proteins undergo hydrolysis, these peptide bonds are broken, resulting in the release of individual amino acids.

2. Dilute Mineral Acids:
- The use of dilute mineral acids (such as hydrochloric acid, HCl) helps in breaking the peptide bonds in proteins through a process known as acid hydrolysis. This process ultimately produces free amino acids.

On the other hand,

A. Sucrose:
- Sucrose is a disaccharide composed of glucose and fructose. It is not related to proteins or their hydrolysis. Sucrose is typically derived from sugarcane or sugar beets and is not a product of protein hydrolysis.

Also,

B. Glucose:
- Glucose is a monosaccharide, a simple sugar that is a primary source of energy for cells. While glucose is a product of carbohydrate hydrolysis (such as starch or glycogen), it is not produced from the hydrolysis of proteins. Proteins do not contain glucose in their structure.

And lastly,

D. Fatty Acids:
- Fatty acids are long hydrocarbon chains typically found in lipids (fats and oils). They are not components of proteins and therefore are not produced from the hydrolysis of proteins. The hydrolysis of lipids, not proteins, yields fatty acids and glycerol.

AgNO₃ (aq) + NaCl (aq) → AgCl (s) + NaNO₃ (aq)

Here, AgCl is the insoluble salt that precipitates out of the solution. Double decomposition is commonly used to prepare many insoluble salts in the laboratory.

What number of moles of oxygen would exert a pressure of 10 atm at 320 K in an 8.2 dm³ cylinder?
[R = 0.082 atm dm³ mol⁻¹ K⁻¹]
A 0.32
B 1.56
C 3.13
D 31.25
The correct answer is Option C. 3.13.

To find the number of moles of oxygen that would exert a pressure of 10 atm in an 8.2 dm³ cylinder at 320 K, we use the ideal gas law equation:

PV = nRT

Where:
- P = Pressure = 10 atm
- V = Volume = 8.2 dm³
- R = Ideal gas constant = 0.082 atm·dm³·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹
- T = Temperature = 320 K

Solving for n:

n = (P × V) / (R × T)

n = (10 × 8.2) / (0.082 × 320)
n = 82 / 26.24
n ≈ 3.13

Therefore, the number of moles of oxygen is approximately 3.13, which corresponds to Option C.

17.
The basic property of salts used as drying agents is
A efflorescence.
B high melting point.
C hygroscopy
D low solubility.
The correct answer is Option C. hygroscopy.

C. Hygroscopy: Hygroscopy refers to the ability of a substance to absorb moisture from the air. Drying agents (desiccants) are used to remove moisture from the surrounding environment. Salts like calcium chloride (CaCl₂) and magnesium sulfate (MgSO₄) are highly hygroscopic, meaning they can effectively attract and hold water molecules, making them suitable for use as drying agents.

On the other hand,

A. Efflorescence: Efflorescence is the process by which a salt migrates to the surface of a porous material and forms a coating. While some hygroscopic salts can exhibit efflorescence, this property is not relevant to their function as drying agents. Efflorescence is more about the deposition of salts rather than moisture absorption.

Also,

B. High Melting Point: A high melting point indicates the temperature at which a solid turns into a liquid. This property is not directly related to the ability of a salt to absorb moisture. Drying agents do not need to have a high melting point to be effective in absorbing moisture.

And lastly,

D. Low Solubility: Low solubility means that a substance does not dissolve easily in water. While low solubility might be advantageous for certain applications, it is not the key property for a drying agent. In fact, many effective drying agents are quite soluble in water (e.g., calcium chloride). The primary function of a drying agent is to absorb moisture, regardless of its solubility.

Under which conditions of pressure (P) and temperature (T) would the volume of an inflated balloon increase? When
A both T and P are increased
B both T and P are decreased
C T is increased and P is decreased
D T is decreased and P is increased
The correct answer is Option C. T is increased and P is decreased.

The correct answer is C. T is increased and P is decreased.

This scenario follows the ideal gas law, which states:

PV = nRT

where P is pressure, V is volume, n is the number of moles of gas, R is the ideal gas constant, and T is temperature in Kelvin.

22.
The collision between ideal gas molecules are considered to be perfectly elastic because
A they collide without losing energy.
B they collide without losing energy.
C their average kinetic energy is variable.
D the distance between them is large compared to their sizes.
The correct answer is Option A. they collide without losing energy.

A. they collide without losing energy: This is the correct answer. In an ideal gas, the molecules are considered to have perfectly elastic collisions because when they collide:

  • Kinetic energy is conserved: The total kinetic energy before and after the collision remains the same.
  • Momentum is conserved: The total momentum before and after collision also remains constant.

This means ideal gas molecules transfer kinetic energy during collisions without converting it into other forms like heat. That’s why Option A is correct.

23.
Elements with high ionization energies would
A lose electrons easily.
B have large atomic radii.
C have high effective nuclear charges.
D have low atomic numbers
The correct answer is Option C. have high effective nuclear charges.

C. have high effective nuclear charges: This is the correct answer.

Ionization energy is the energy required to remove an electron from an atom or ion in the gaseous state. Elements with high ionization energies require more energy to remove an electron because they have a strong hold on their electrons. This typically occurs when:

  • Effective nuclear charge is high: The net positive charge experienced by an electron in an atom is stronger due to more protons and less shielding. Outer electrons are tightly held.
  • Atomic radius is small: Outer electrons are closer to the nucleus and feel stronger attraction, making them harder to remove.

Therefore, elements with high ionization energies generally have high effective nuclear charges (Option C), making it difficult to remove electrons from them.

24.
Which of the following statements about Group VII elements is correct?
A They are present in the same physical state
B They are strong reducing agents.
C Their reactivity decreases down the group
D They exist as monoatomic molecules
The correct answer is Option C. Their reactivity decreases down the group.

C. Their reactivity decreases down the group: Group VII elements, also known as the halogens (F, Cl, Br, I, At), all have seven electrons in their outermost shell, making them very reactive nonmetals. However, as you move down the group, their reactivity decreases because:

  • Atomic size: Increases down the group, making it harder to attract new electrons.
  • Shielding effect: Inner electrons block outer electrons from the nucleus, reducing attraction.
  • Bond dissociation energy: Decreases, weakening their ability to form reactive bonds.

So, the correct answer is that reactivity decreases down the group.

A. They are present in the same physical state: Incorrect. Halogens exist in different physical states — e.g., fluorine and chlorine are gases, bromine is a liquid, and iodine is a solid at room temperature.

B. They are strong reducing agents: Incorrect. Halogens are actually strong oxidizing agents, not reducing agents.

D. They exist as monoatomic molecules: Incorrect. Halogens exist as diatomic molecules (e.g., Cl2, F2, I2).

30.
At 25 °C, evaporation of a 100 cm3 solution of K2CO3 to dryness gave 14 g of the salt. What is the solubility of K2CO3 at 25 °C? [K2CO3 = 138]
A 0.01 mol dm-3
B 0.101 mol dm-3
C 1.01 mol dm-3
D 10.0 mol dm-3
The correct answer is Option C. 1.01 mol dm-3.

Explanation:

To find the solubility of K2CO3 at 25°C:

  1. Calculate the molar mass of K2CO3:
    Molar mass = 2 × K (39.1) + C (12.0) + 3 × O (16.0) = 78.2 + 12.0 + 48.0 = 138.2 g/mol
  2. Convert mass to moles:
    Moles = 14 g / 138.2 g/mol ≈ 0.101 mol
  3. Convert 100 cm³ to dm³:
    100 cm³ = 0.1 dm³
  4. Calculate solubility:
    Solubility = 0.101 mol / 0.1 dm³ = 1.01 mol dm-3

Therefore, the solubility of K2CO3 at 25°C is 1.01 mol dm-3.

35.
Which of the following statements is correct about ionization energy? It
A decreases across the periods
B results in the formation of an anion
C causes metallic nuclei to disintegrate
D decreases down the group
The correct answer is Option D. decreases down the group.

Ionization energy refers to the energy required to remove an electron from an atom or ion in its gaseous state.

D. decreases down the group: This is the correct statement. Ionization energy generally decreases down a group because:

  • The outermost electrons are farther from the nucleus
  • They're shielded by inner electrons
