1.

Which of the following quantities is not an example of force?

A. Mass
B. Tension
C. Weight.
D. Friction.
The quantity that is not an example of force among the given options is A. Mass. Here’s why: Force is a vector quantity that has both magnitude and direction. It is a push or pull upon an object resulting from the object’s interaction with another object. Examples of force include tension, weight, and friction. Mass, on the other hand, is a scalar quantity that measures the amount of matter in an object. It is not a force but rather a measure of the resistance of an object to acceleration when a net force is applied. It remains constant regardless of where the object is in the universe. So, mass is not a force but a property of matter. It’s the amount of matter in an object, while force is an interaction that changes the motion of an object.
