7.

There was an agitation for the….. law on libel to be re-enacted.

A. Enforced
B. Repealed
C. Rejected
D. Cancelled
The correct answer is Option B. Repealed.



The sentence reads: "There was an agitation for the ____ law on libel to be re-enacted." To understand this sentence, we need to break down the key terms and concepts: 1. Agitation: This means a state of anxiety or nervous excitement, often leading to public demands or protests. 2. Re-enacted: This means to enact (a law) again. The context implies that people are demanding that a law on libel, which has already been enacted once, be enacted again. This suggests that the law must have been abolished or annulled in some way, and now there is a desire to bring it back. Repealed - means to revoke or annul a law. If a law was repealed, it was canceled or abolished, and re-enacting it would mean bringing it back.

On the other hand,

A. Enforced - means to make people obey a law, which doesn't fit because a law that needs to be re-enacted isn't currently in effect and thus can't be enforced.

Also,
C. Rejected - means to dismiss or refuse. This doesn't fit well in the context because rejecting a law doesn't necessarily imply it was once in effect.
And lastly,
D. Canceled - similar to repealed, but "repealed" is the more precise legal term used when referring to the annulment of laws.
