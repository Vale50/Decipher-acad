3a(i) What is an ecological niche? An ecological niche is the role that a species plays in its environment. It includes the species' physical requirements, such as its habitat and food sources, as well as its interactions with other species. 3a(ii) State five roles played by a mango tree in its ecological niche. Food source: The mango tree provides food for a variety of animals, including humans, monkeys, and bats. Habitat: The mango tree provides shelter for a variety of animals, including birds, insects, and reptiles. Pollinator: The mango tree is pollinated by bees, butterflies, and other insects. Water filter: The mango tree's roots help to filter water, making it cleaner for other plants and animals to use. Carbon sink: The mango tree absorbs carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, helping to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

(b) (i) State two reasons why ecologists use sampling techniques in population studies. (ii) State four examples of sampling techniques used in population studies.

(3b)(i) To save time and money: Sampling techniques allow ecologists to study a small number of individuals from a population and make inferences about the entire population. To reduce the impact of human disturbance: Sampling techniques can help to reduce the impact of human disturbance on a population, such as by avoiding the need to capture and mark individuals. 3b(ii) Quadrat sampling: This technique involves dividing the study area into a grid of squares and counting the number of individuals in each square. Line transect sampling: This technique involves walking along a straight line and counting the number of individuals that cross the line. Point sampling: This technique involves randomly placing points in the study area and counting the number of individuals that are within a certain distance of each point. Canopy cover sampling: This technique involves measuring the amount of sunlight that reaches the ground in different parts of the study area. This can be used to estimate the density of trees in the area.

(c) Name four types of fingerprints in humans

Combinations: Combinations are fingerprints that have a mixture of arch, loop, and whorl patterns.
