1.
The instrument used to measure relative humidity is a
A Barometer
B Hydrometer
C Hygrometer
D Manometer
The correct answer is Option C: Hygrometer.

A hygrometer is an instrument used to measure the relative humidity in the atmosphere. It measures the amount of water vapor present in the air.

Why "Barometer" is incorrect:

A barometer is used to measure atmospheric pressure, not humidity. It helps in weather forecasting and altitude measurement.

Why "Hydrometer" is incorrect:

A hydrometer is used to measure the relative density or specific gravity of liquids, not humidity in the air.

Why "Manometer" is incorrect:

A manometer is used to measure the pressure of gases and liquids, particularly in closed systems, not humidity.

More About Humidity Measurement

Relative humidity is the ratio of the current absolute humidity to the highest possible absolute humidity (which depends on the current air temperature). A reading of 100% relative humidity means that the air is totally saturated with water vapor and cannot hold any more.

Types of Hygrometers:

  • Psychrometer: Uses two thermometers (wet and dry bulb) to determine humidity
  • Hair Tension Hygrometer: Uses human or animal hair which changes length with humidity
  • Electronic Hygrometer: Uses electronic sensors to measure moisture content
  • Dew Point Hygrometer: Measures the temperature at which dew forms

Understanding humidity is important in many fields including meteorology, agriculture, manufacturing, and building construction.

2.
Which part of the human eye performs the same function as the diaphragm in a lens camera?
A Choroid
B Iris
C Cornea
D Retina
The correct answer is Option B: Iris.

The iris controls the amount of light entering the eye by adjusting the size of the pupil, just as a camera's diaphragm controls the amount of light reaching the film or sensor.

Why "Choroid" is incorrect:

The choroid is a layer of blood vessels that provides nourishment to the eye. It does not control light entry like a camera diaphragm.

Why "Cornea" is incorrect:

The cornea is the transparent front part of the eye that covers the iris and pupil. It refracts light but doesn't control the amount of light entering the eye.

Why "Retina" is incorrect:

The retina is the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye that detects light, similar to film in a camera, not the diaphragm.

Eye-Camera Analogies

The human eye and a camera share many similarities in how they process images:

  • Iris ↔ Diaphragm: Both control the amount of light entering
  • Pupil ↔ Aperture: The opening through which light passes
  • Lens ↔ Camera Lens: Both focus light onto the image-sensing surface
  • Retina ↔ Film/Sensor: Where the image is captured
  • Cornea ↔ Glass Cover: Protective transparent covering

In bright light, the iris contracts to make the pupil smaller, reducing light entry. In dim light, it dilates to allow more light in. This automatic adjustment is similar to how a camera's diaphragm adjusts aperture size.

3.
An electron moves from an energy level of -8.6 eV to another level of -3.5 eV. Determine the frequency of the absorbed radiation. [1 eV = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹J, h = 6.6 × 10⁻³⁴Js]
A 1.24 × 10¹⁵Hz
B 2.93 × 10¹⁵Hz
C 3.59 × 10¹⁶Hz
D 5.39 × 10¹⁶Hz
The correct answer is Option A: 1.24 × 10¹⁵Hz.

Solution:

Energy difference: ΔE = E₂ - E₁ = -3.5 - (-8.6) = 5.1 eV

Converting to Joules: ΔE = 5.1 × 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ = 8.16 × 10⁻¹⁹ J

Using E = hf, where f is frequency:

f = E/h = (8.16 × 10⁻¹⁹)/(6.6 × 10⁻³⁴) = 1.24 × 10¹⁵ Hz

Why "2.93 × 10¹⁵Hz" is incorrect:

This answer suggests an error in the calculation, possibly using the wrong energy difference or conversion factor.

Why "3.59 × 10¹⁶Hz" is incorrect:

This value is too high and suggests an error in the calculation, possibly missing the correct power of 10.

Why "5.39 × 10¹⁶Hz" is incorrect:

This frequency is much too high for the given energy transition and suggests fundamental calculation errors.

Energy Transitions and Photon Emission

When an electron transitions between energy levels in an atom, it either absorbs or emits a photon. The energy of this photon is equal to the difference in energy between the two levels.

Key Concepts:

  • Energy-Frequency Relationship: E = hf, where h is Planck's constant
  • Absorption: Electron moves to higher (less negative) energy level
  • Emission: Electron moves to lower (more negative) energy level
  • Energy Conservation: ΔE = hf

In this problem, the electron moves from -8.6 eV to -3.5 eV, which is an upward transition (absorption). The energy absorbed is 5.1 eV, which corresponds to ultraviolet radiation with a frequency of 1.24 × 10¹⁵ Hz.
