The tricuspid valve is located between the?

A. left auricle and left ventricle
B. right auricle and the superior vena cava
C. aorta and the left ventricle.
D. right auricle and right ventricle
The correct answer is Option D. right auricle and right ventricle



**D. right auricle and right ventricle**: This is correct. The tricuspid valve is located between the right atrium (auricle) and the right ventricle. It prevents backflow of blood from the right ventricle into the right atrium when the ventricle contracts.

On the other hand,
**A. left auricle and left ventricle**: This is incorrect. The valve located between the left auricle (atrium) and the left ventricle is the mitral valve (also known as the bicuspid valve), not the tricuspid valve.

Also,
**B. right auricle and the superior vena cava**: This is incorrect. The superior vena cava is a major vein that brings deoxygenated blood from the upper part of the body to the right atrium (auricle). There is no valve named the tricuspid valve between the right auricle and the superior vena cava.

And lastly,
**C. aorta and the left ventricle**: This is incorrect. The valve located between the left ventricle and the aorta is the aortic valve. The tricuspid valve is not located here.

Which of the following hormones is wrongly paired with its secretory organ?

A. Insulin and pancreas
B. Oestrogen and ovary
C. Oxytocin and hypothalamus
D. Adrenalin and kidney
The correct answer is Option D. Adrenalin and kidney.



**D. Adrenalin and kidney**: This is incorrect. Adrenalin (also known as epinephrine) is produced by the adrenal glands, which are located on top of the kidneys, but not by the kidneys themselves. The adrenal glands are responsible for producing several hormones, including adrenaline, which plays a key role in the fight-or-flight response.

On the other hand,
**A. Insulin and pancreas**: This is correct. Insulin is a hormone produced by the beta cells of the islets of Langerhans in the pancreas. It helps regulate blood glucose levels by facilitating the uptake of glucose into cells.

Also,
**B. Oestrogen and ovary**: This is correct. Oestrogen is a hormone produced primarily by the ovaries in females. It is responsible for the development and regulation of the female reproductive system and secondary sexual characteristics.

And lastly,
**C. Oxytocin and hypothalamus**: This is incorrect. Oxytocin is indeed produced by the hypothalamus, but it is secreted by the posterior pituitary gland. The hypothalamus synthesizes oxytocin, which is then transported to and released from the posterior pituitary gland into the bloodstream.

Which of the following processes does not occur during photosynthesis?

A. Carbon (IV) oxide is evolved
B. water is split
C. Oxygen is evolved
D. Energy from sunlight is absorbed
The correct answer is Option A. Carbon (IV) oxide is evolved.



**A. Carbon (IV) oxide is evolved**: This statement is incorrect because during photosynthesis, carbon dioxide (CO2) is actually consumed from the atmosphere to produce glucose and oxygen (O2). Therefore, carbon dioxide is not evolved or released during photosynthesis.

On the other hand,
**B. Water is split**: This process, known as photolysis or light-dependent reactions, occurs during photosynthesis. Water molecules are split into oxygen, electrons, and protons (hydrogen ions) in the thylakoid membranes of chloroplasts

Also,
**C. Oxygen is evolved**: Oxygen (O2) is indeed evolved as a byproduct of photosynthesis during the light-dependent reactions, specifically from the photolysis of water.

And lastly,
**D. Energy from sunlight is absorbed**: This is a fundamental process in photosynthesis where chlorophyll and other pigments absorb photons (light energy) from sunlight. This absorbed energy is used to drive the conversion of carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen.

Stunted growth and small yellow leaves in plants are symptoms of the deficiencies of?

A. sulphur.
B.iron
C. magnesium
D. nitrogen
The correct answer is Option D. nitrogen.



**D. Nitrogen**: Nitrogen deficiency is characterized by stunted growth and small, yellow leaves (chlorosis), especially in older leaves. This is because nitrogen is crucial for the synthesis of chlorophyll and proteins in plants.

On the other hand,
**A. Sulphur**: Sulphur deficiency typically causes yellowing of leaves, especially the younger leaves, but it does not generally lead to stunted growth. Sulphur is essential for protein synthesis and enzyme function in plants.

Also,
**B. Iron**: Iron deficiency causes interveinal chlorosis, where the veins of leaves remain green but the tissue between them turns yellow. It can also lead to stunted growth if severe, but it does not usually cause small yellow leaves.

And lastly,
**C. Magnesium**: Magnesium deficiency also causes interveinal chlorosis, similar to iron deficiency, but magnesium deficiency symptoms start from older leaves. Stunted growth can occur in severe cases, but small yellow leaves are not a typical symptom.

Organisms that feed on dead, decaying tissues of other organisms are known as

The correct answer is Option D. saprophytes.

The type of nutrition in which organisms take in solid organic materials into their body is

A. holozoic.
B. symbiotic
C. saprophytic.
D. parasitic
The correct answer is Option A. holozoic.



**A. Holozoic**: This type of nutrition involves the ingestion of whole or large pieces of solid organic material by an organism. Organisms that exhibit holozoic nutrition include animals and many protists. After ingestion, the food is broken down through mechanical and enzymatic processes inside the organism's digestive system to release nutrients for absorption.

On the other hand,
**B. Symbiotic**: Symbiotic nutrition refers to a mutually beneficial relationship between different species where at least one species derives nutrients or other benefits from the association. Examples include lichens (mutualistic association between fungi and algae) and nitrogen-fixing bacteria in legume roots.

Also,
**C. Saprophytic**: Saprophytic nutrition involves obtaining nutrients from dead and decaying organic matter. Organisms that exhibit saprophytic nutrition, such as fungi and many bacteria, secrete enzymes that break down complex organic materials externally into simpler substances, which are then absorbed by the organism.

And lastly,
**D. Parasitic**: Parasitic nutrition involves one organism (parasite) living in or on another organism (host) and obtaining nutrients at the expense of the host. Parasites typically harm their hosts to varying degrees, ranging from mild to severe, depending on the relationship.

A Rhesus negative woman may experience stillbirth of the second child that is Rhesus positive primarily because of the immune reaction known as Rh incompatibility. Here's an explanation: - **B. the blood of the foetus might clump**: This is the correct answer. In Rh incompatibility, if a Rhesus negative woman (who lacks the Rh antigen on her red blood cells) carries a Rhesus positive baby (who has the Rh antigen inherited from the father), her immune system may recognize the Rh antigen on the baby's red blood cells as foreign. This can lead to the production of antibodies against the Rh antigen. These antibodies can cross the placenta and attack the red blood cells of the baby, causing them to clump together (agglutinate) and be destroyed. This immune response can result in severe hemolytic disease of the newborn (HDN) in subsequent pregnancies, leading to stillbirth or neonatal death.

On the other hand,
**A. the woman is susceptible to diseases during pregnancy**: While maternal health can influence pregnancy outcomes, Rh incompatibility specifically relates to the immune response against the Rh antigen.

Also,
**C. they have poor circulatory system**: This option does not relate to the specific mechanism of Rh incompatibility.

And lastly,
**D. the foetus is deprived of nourishment**: Rh incompatibility does not directly cause deprivation of nourishment to the fetus; it affects the red blood cells' destruction due to the immune response.
