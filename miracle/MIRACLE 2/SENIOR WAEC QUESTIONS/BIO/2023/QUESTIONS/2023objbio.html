1.
The ability of organisms to maintain a constant internal environment is known as
A diuresis
B endosmosis
C homeostasis
D plasmolysis
The correct answer is Option C: homeostasis.

Homeostasis refers to the process by which organisms maintain a stable internal environment despite changes in external conditions.

Why "diuresis" is incorrect:

Diuresis refers to increased or excessive production of urine, which is just one specific bodily process and not the overall maintenance of internal stability.

Why "endosmosis" is incorrect:

Endosmosis refers to the movement of a solvent into a cell or an area of lower concentration, which is a specific physical process and not the overall regulation of an organism's internal environment.

Why "plasmolysis" is incorrect:

Plasmolysis refers to the process in which cells lose water in a hypertonic solution, causing the cell membrane to pull away from the cell wall. This is a specific cellular response, not the broader concept of maintaining internal balance.

More About Homeostasis

Homeostasis is a fundamental concept in biology that refers to the ability of an organism to maintain stable internal conditions necessary for survival, despite changes in the external environment.

Examples of homeostatic processes include:

  • Temperature regulation
  • Blood glucose regulation
  • Fluid and electrolyte balance
  • pH regulation
  • Blood pressure regulation

Each of these processes involves sensors that detect changes, control centers that process information, and effectors that correct imbalances through negative feedback mechanisms.

The diagram below is an illustration of a mango leaf drawn by a student in a Biology test. The student failed the test. Study it and answer questions 2 and 3.
The likely reason why the student failed the test was that the
A labels were wrong and the guidelines were not touching the structures
B net venation shown were few, rough, not properly drawn and wrongly placed
C leaf was too tapered at the apex, the leaf stalk was too short and the shape of leaf was wrong
D leaf venation should have been parallel and the midrib should be thicker
The correct answer is Option A: labels were wrong and the guidelines were not touching the structures.

When drawing biological diagrams, it's essential that labels are accurate and that guidelines properly touch the structures they're identifying.

Why Option B is incorrect:

While proper venation is important in leaf diagrams, this is typically a secondary concern compared to correct labeling and guidelines.

Why Option C is incorrect:

Leaf shape details are important but minor inaccuracies here would rarely be the primary reason for failing a biology drawing test.

Why Option D is incorrect:

Mango leaves have net venation, not parallel venation. Parallel venation is characteristic of monocot plants like grasses, corn, and lilies, whereas mango is a dicot with net venation.

Proper Biological Drawing Techniques

In biological drawings, several key principles should be followed:

  • Clear outlines: Drawings should have clear, continuous lines.
  • Proper proportions: The relative sizes of different parts should be accurate.
  • Correct labeling: Labels must be accurate and placed horizontally.
  • Guidelines: Straight lines that connect labels to the structures they identify.
  • No shading: Biological drawings typically avoid shading or coloring.
  • Title: A descriptive title should be included.
  • Magnification: The scale of the drawing should be indicated.

Failing to follow these principles, especially regarding proper labeling and guidelines, can result in significant point deductions in assessments.

The tricuspid valve is located between the?
A left auricle and left ventricle.
B right auricle and the superior vena cava.
C aorta and the left ventricle.
D right auricle and right ventricle.
The correct answer is Option D. right auricle and right ventricle.

D. right auricle and right ventricle: This is correct. The tricuspid valve is located between the right atrium (auricle) and the right ventricle. It prevents backflow of blood from the right ventricle into the right atrium when the ventricle contracts.


On the other hand,

A. left auricle and left ventricle This is incorrect. The valve located between the left auricle (atrium) and the left ventricle is the mitral valve (also known as the bicuspid valve), not the tricuspid valve.

B. right auricle and the superior vena cava: This is incorrect. The superior vena cava is a major vein that brings deoxygenated blood from the upper part of the body to the right atrium (auricle). There is no valve named the tricuspid valve between the right auricle and the superior vena cava.

C. aorta and the left ventricle: This is incorrect. The valve located between the left ventricle and the aorta is the aortic valve. The tricuspid valve is not located here.

22.
Stunted growth and small yellow leaves in plants are symptoms of the deficiencies of?
A sulphur
B iron
C magnesium
D nitrogen
The correct answer is Option D. nitrogen.

D. Nitrogen: Nitrogen deficiency is characterized by stunted growth and small, yellow leaves (chlorosis), especially in older leaves. This is because nitrogen is crucial for the synthesis of chlorophyll and proteins in plants.

On the other hand,

A. Sulphur: Sulphur deficiency typically causes yellowing of leaves, especially the younger leaves, but it does not generally lead to stunted growth. Sulphur is essential for protein synthesis and enzyme function in plants.

Also,

B. Iron: Iron deficiency causes interveinal chlorosis, where the veins of leaves remain green but the tissue between them turns yellow. It can also lead to stunted growth if severe, but it does not usually cause small yellow leaves.

And lastly,

C. Magnesium: Magnesium deficiency also causes interveinal chlorosis, similar to iron deficiency, but magnesium deficiency symptoms start from older leaves. Stunted growth can occur in severe cases, but small yellow leaves are not a typical symptom.

D. Protein: Proteins are essential for growth, repair, and maintaining body tissues. They can also be used for energy, but like amino acids, they yield about 4 kcal of energy per gram.

The correct answer is Option D. saprophytes.

On the other hand,

A. Parasites: Parasites are organisms that live in or on another organism (the host) and benefit at the host's expense. They typically derive nutrients directly from the host's tissues or body fluids.

Also,

B. Autotrophs: Autotrophs are organisms that can produce their own food using energy from sunlight (photosynthesis) or from inorganic compounds (chemosynthesis). They do not rely on consuming other organisms for nutrients.

And lastly,

C. Heterotrophs: Heterotrophs are organisms that obtain their nutrients and energy by consuming other organisms or organic matter. This category includes animals, fungi, and some bacteria.

Which of the following factors is the main problem facing xerophytes?
A Conservation of excess water
B Competition for sunlight
C Lack of adequate water
D Poor formation of roots
The correct answer is Option C. Lack of adequate water.

C. Lack of adequate water: This is the correct answer. Xerophytes face a constant challenge due to the scarcity of water in their habitats. Their adaptations, such as reduced leaf surface area, thick cuticles, and deep root systems, are aimed at coping with this lack of water.

On the other hand,

A. Conservation of excess water: While xerophytes are efficient in conserving water, their primary concern is not the conservation of excess water but rather obtaining enough water to survive in arid or semi-arid conditions.

Also,

B. Competition for sunlight: While competition for sunlight can be a challenge for all plants, it is not specific to xerophytes. Xerophytes are more concerned with water availability rather than sunlight.

And lastly,

D. Poor formation of roots: While root development is important for water uptake, xerophytes often have adaptations that enhance their root systems to maximize water absorption from the soil. The main issue they face, however, is the availability of water rather than the formation of roots.

The correct answer is Option C. heterotrophic.

On the other hand,

And lastly,

D. Autotrophic: Autotrophic organisms produce their own organic molecules using inorganic substances like carbon dioxide and water, usually through photosynthesis or chemosynthesis. Pitcher plants do not produce their own food from inorganic sources; they trap insects instead.

Which of the following characteristics shows discontinuous variation in plants?
A colour of flower
B Height of plant
C leaf size
D fruit size
The correct answer is Option A. colour of flower.

A. colour of flower: Discontinuous variation refers to traits that have distinct categories or groups with no intermediate forms. In plants, the color of flowers is a classic example of discontinuous variation because flowers typically exhibit clear-cut differences in color, such as red, yellow, blue, etc. There are no intermediate colors; a flower is either one color or another.

On the other hand,

B. Height of plant: Plant height usually shows continuous variation, where plants can vary incrementally from very short to very tall without distinct categories or groups.

Also,

C. Leaf size: Leaf size also tends to vary continuously, with leaves ranging in size along a spectrum rather than falling into distinct categories.

And lastly,

D. Fruit size: Fruit size, like plant height and leaf size, generally varies continuously. Fruits can vary in size gradually without distinct categories.

A Rhesus negative woman may experience stillbirth of the second child that is Rhesus positive because
A the woman is susceptible to diseases during pregnancy.
B the blood of the foetus might clump
C they have poor circulatory system.
D the foetus is deprived of nourishment
The correct answer is Option B. the blood of the foetus might clump.

A Rhesus negative woman may experience stillbirth of the second child that is Rhesus positive primarily because of the immune reaction known as Rh incompatibility. Here's an explanation:

B. the blood of the foetus might clump: This is the correct answer. In Rh incompatibility, if a Rhesus negative woman (who lacks the Rh antigen on her red blood cells) carries a Rhesus positive baby (who has the Rh antigen inherited from the father), her immune system may recognize the Rh antigen on the baby's red blood cells as foreign. This can lead to the production of antibodies against the Rh antigen. These antibodies can cross the placenta and attack the red blood cells of the baby, causing them to clump together (agglutinate) and be destroyed. This immune response can result in severe hemolytic disease of the newborn (HDN) in subsequent pregnancies, leading to stillbirth or neonatal death.

On the other hand,

A. the woman is susceptible to diseases during pregnancy: While maternal health can influence pregnancy outcomes, Rh incompatibility specifically relates to the immune response against the Rh antigen.

Also,

C. they have poor circulatory system: This option does not relate to the specific mechanism of Rh incompatibility.

And lastly,

D. the foetus is deprived of nourishment: Rh incompatibility does not directly cause deprivation of nourishment to the fetus; it affects the red blood cells' destruction due to the immune response.

A sudden change in the genetic composition of an organism is termed?
A epistasis
B linkage
C mutation
D variation
The correct answer is Option C. mutation.

C. mutation: A sudden change in the genetic composition of an organism is termed mutation. Mutations are alterations in the DNA sequence that can lead to changes in the phenotype (observable traits) of an organism. They are the primary source of genetic variation, and they can occur spontaneously or be induced by various factors such as radiation, chemicals, or errors in DNA replication.

On the other hand,

A. epistasis: Epistasis refers to the interaction between different genes where one gene masks or modifies the expression of another gene.

Also,

B. linkage: Linkage refers to the tendency of genes that are located close together on a chromosome to be inherited together during the process of genetic recombination.

And lastly,

D. variation: Variation refers to the differences in traits among individuals of the same species, which can arise due to genetic factors, environmental factors, or a combination of both.

What is the probability that a pregnancy would result in a male child?
A 1
B ¾
C ½
D ¼
The correct answer is Option C. ½

C. ½: The probability that a pregnancy would result in a male child is influenced by biological factors. In humans, the sex of a child is determined by the combination of sex chromosomes inherited from the parents. Typically, a fertilized egg (zygote) will develop into either a male (with XY chromosomes) or a female (with XX chromosomes).

Since the genetic contribution from the father determines the sex of the child, the probability of having a male child is theoretically 1 in 2, or ½, which is equivalent to 0.5 or 50%.

This means that for any given pregnancy, there is an equal chance that the child will be male or female. Therefore, the correct answer is C. ½ (or 50%).
