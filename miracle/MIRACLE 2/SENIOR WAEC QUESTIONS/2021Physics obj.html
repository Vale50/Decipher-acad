Which of the following quantities is not an example of force?

A. Mass
B. Tension
C. Weight.
D. Friction.
The quantity that is not an example of force among the given options is A. Mass.



**Mass** is a measure of the amount of matter in an object, typically measured in kilograms (kg). It is a scalar quantity and does not have a direction. Mass is not a force; rather, it is a measure of the inertia of an object, or how much it resists acceleration when a force is applied.

Option B. **Tension** is a force that is transmitted through a string, rope, cable, or similar object when it is pulled tight by forces acting from opposite ends. It is a vector quantity, having both magnitude and direction.

Also,
Option C. **Weight** is the force exerted on a mass by gravity. It is calculated as the mass of an object multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity (Weight = Mass Ã— g). Weight is also a vector quantity, directed towards the center of the Earth.
And lastly,
Option D. **Friction** is the force that opposes the relative motion or tendency of such motion of two surfaces in contact. It acts parallel to the surfaces in contact and is also a vector quantity. Therefore, mass is the correct answer as it is not a force but rather a property of matter.

The reason for laminating the soft iron core of a transformer is to _____

A. Reduce eddy current
B. Reduce the magnetic flux
C. Increase the magnetic flux
D. Reduce its efficiency
The correct answer is Option A. Reduce eddy current. The reason for laminating the soft iron core of a transformer is to **reduce eddy current**.



Laminating the soft iron core helps to reduce the formation of eddy currents. Eddy currents are loops of electric current induced within the core due to the changing magnetic field. These currents can generate significant heat and result in energy losses within the core. By laminating the core, the eddy currents are confined to smaller, thinner sections, significantly reducing their magnitude and thus minimizing the associated energy losses. This improves the overall efficiency of the transformer.

On the other hand,

Option B. The purpose of the core laminations is not to reduce the magnetic flux. In fact, the core is designed to efficiently guide the magnetic flux through the transformer. Reducing the magnetic flux would decrease the transformer's effectiveness in transferring energy from the primary to the secondary coil.

Also,
Option C. While the core material itself is chosen to have high magnetic permeability to increase the magnetic flux, laminating the core is specifically done to reduce eddy currents, not to increase the magnetic flux. The lamination does not directly affect the amount of magnetic flux but rather reduces losses associated with eddy currents. .
And lastly,
Option D. This option is incorrect because laminating the core actually helps to **increase** the efficiency of the transformer. By reducing eddy current losses, the overall efficiency of the transformer is improved, as less energy is wasted as heat. In summary, laminating the soft iron core of a transformer is primarily done to reduce eddy currents, which in turn helps to improve the efficiency of the transformer. The other options either misunderstand the role of lamination or incorrectly state its effects on the transformer's operation.

Formation of hydrogen bubbles at the copper plate of a primary cell is called

A. Polarization
B. Back e.m.f.
C. Electrolysis
D. Local action
The correct answer is A. Polarization. The formation of hydrogen bubbles at the copper plate of a primary cell is known as **polarization**.



vPolarization - **Definition**: Polarization in the context of primary cells refers to the build-up of hydrogen gas bubbles on the surface of the electrode (usually the cathode). - **Effect**: This build-up of hydrogen can hinder the flow of electrons, effectively increasing the internal resistance of the cell and reducing its efficiency. - **Cause**: It occurs because the hydrogen ions (protons) in the electrolyte gain electrons at the cathode and form hydrogen gas.

On the other hand,

Option B. Back e.m.f.**: - **Definition**: Back electromotive force (e.m.f.) refers to the voltage generated by a running motor or generator that opposes the applied voltage. - **Relevance**: This term is more relevant to electric motors and generators, not the formation of hydrogen bubbles in a primary cell.

Also,
Option C. **Electrolysis**: - **Definition**: Electrolysis is a process that uses an electric current to drive a non-spontaneous chemical reaction. - **Relevance**: While hydrogen can be produced during electrolysis, this process involves applying an external voltage to drive the reaction, rather than being a side effect in a primary cell.
And lastly,
Option D. **Local Action**: - **Definition**: Local action refers to the unwanted consumption of the active material of a battery's electrode due to impurities, leading to self-discharge even when the battery is not supplying current. - **Relevance**: Local action affects the durability and lifespan of the battery but is different from the specific phenomenon of hydrogen gas build-up on the cathode. ### Conclusion The formation of hydrogen bubbles at the copper plate of a primary cell is correctly termed **polarization** because it specifically describes the effect of hydrogen gas accumulation on the electrode, which impacts the cell's performance by increasing resistance and reducing efficiency.

On the other hand,

Option A. Temperature**: - **Effect**: Increasing the temperature increases the kinetic energy of the liquid molecules. This results in more molecules having enough energy to escape the surface and transition into the vapor phase. - **Reason**: Higher temperatures provide the necessary energy for more molecules to overcome intermolecular forces and evaporate.

Also,
Option C. Wind**: - **Effect**: Wind increases the rate of evaporation by removing the vapor molecules from the liquid surface, preventing them from condensing back into the liquid. - **Reason**: This creates a gradient that allows more liquid molecules to escape into the air, enhancing the evaporation process.
And lastly,
Option D. Surface Area**: - **Effect**: Increasing the surface area of the liquid exposes more molecules to the air, facilitating more opportunities for evaporation. - **Reason**: A larger surface area allows more molecules to escape from the liquid phase at any given time

In the context of the question, the opening through which light passes to reach the retina is specifically the **pupil**. 6.

A heat sensitive resistor made of a semiconductor is called a

A.Thermometer
B.Thermostat
C. Thermistor
D.Thermopile
The correct answer is Option C. Thermistor.



A thermistor is a type of resistor whose resistance varies significantly with temperature. Thermistors are made from semiconductor materials, and they are highly sensitive to temperature changes. The resistance of a thermistor decreases as the temperature increases in a Negative Temperature Coefficient (NTC) thermistor, and increases as the temperature increases in a Positive Temperature Coefficient (PTC) thermistor.

On the other hand,

Option A. Thermometer** - A thermometer is an instrument used to measure temperature. It does not function as a resistor and does not change its resistance with temperature. Instead, it often uses substances like mercury, alcohol, or digital sensors to provide a temperature reading. It is not made from semiconductor materials and is not used as a resistor in electronic circuits.

Also,
Option B. A thermostat is a device that regulates the temperature of a system. It does so by switching heating or cooling devices on or off to maintain a desired temperature. While a thermostat might contain a thermistor to measure temperature, it itself is not a heat-sensitive resistor. It is a control device rather than a component like a resistor.
And lastly,
Option D. Thermopile** - A thermopile is an electronic device that converts thermal energy into electrical energy. It is made up of several thermocouples connected usually in series or parallel. Thermopiles are used for measuring temperature differences and generating electricity from heat, but they are not resistors and do not change resistance with temperature in the same way a thermistor does. In summary, the correct answer is **C. Thermistor** because it is a heat-sensitive resistor made of semiconductor materials, which directly responds to changes in temperature by altering its resistance. The other options describe different devices or instruments related to temperature measurement or control but do not fit the description of a heat-sensitive resistor.
