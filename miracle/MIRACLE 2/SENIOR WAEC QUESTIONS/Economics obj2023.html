1.

The study of economics is mainly concerned with how to

A. Rank individuals’ wants given the abundant resources
B. Make choice when resources are inadequate
C. Satisfy every member of all societies.
D. Produce all the goods needed by everyone.
The correct answer is Option B. make choice when resources are inadequate



The correct answer is B. make choice when resources are inadequate. Explanation: Economics is fundamentally the study of how individuals, businesses, and societies make choices in the face of scarcity. Scarcity refers to the basic economic problem that resources (such as time, money, raw materials, etc.) are limited, while human wants and needs are virtually unlimited. Because of this, economics focuses on how to allocate these limited resources to meet the most pressing needs and desires.

On the other hand,
A. rank individuals’ wants given the abundant resources: This option is incorrect because it assumes that resources are abundant, which contradicts the central concept of scarcity in economics. If resources were abundant, there would be no need to rank wants or make difficult choices.

Also,
C. satisfy every member of all societies: This option is incorrect because it is impossible to satisfy every member of society given the limited resources available. Economics instead studies how to make the best possible choices to maximize welfare given these constraints.

And lastly,
D. produce all the goods needed by everyone: This option is incorrect because it assumes that it's possible to produce everything needed by everyone, which ignores the reality of limited resources. Economics involves making decisions about what goods and services to produce, how to produce them, and for whom they should be produced.
2.

Warehousing is an economic activity that falls under

A. Nertiary production.
B. Intermediate production.
C. Secondary production.
D. Primary production.
The correct answer is Option A. tertiary production



Tertiary Production: This sector involves services that support the primary and secondary sectors and includes activities like transportation, communication, and warehousing. Warehousing is considered a part of the tertiary sector because it provides a service that supports the storage and distribution of goods.

On the other hand,
B. Intermediate Production: This term is not commonly used as a separate production category in economic activity. It may refer to the stages between primary and secondary production but does not accurately describe warehousing.

Also,
C. Secondary Production: This sector involves manufacturing and processing, where raw materials from the primary sector are converted into finished goods. Warehousing is not part of the production process itself but is a service that aids in the distribution of goods after they have been produced.

And lastly,
D. Primary Production: This sector includes activities related to the extraction and harvesting of natural resources, such as farming, mining, and fishing. Warehousing does not fall under this category, as it does not involve the extraction or direct use of natural resources.
3.

Which of the following items is not classified as working capital?

A. Equipment
B. Fuel
C. Money to pay wages
D. Semi-finished goods
The correct answer is Option A. Equipment



A. Equipment is not classified as working capital because it is a long-term asset. Working capital typically includes assets that are short-term and can be quickly converted into cash or are used in day-to-day operations, such as cash, inventory, and accounts receivable. Equipment is considered a fixed or capital asset, used over a longer period to generate revenue.

On the other hand,
B. Fuel is classified as working capital because it is consumed in the day-to-day operations of a business and is part of the operating expenses. It is a short-term asset that is regularly used up in the production process or service delivery.

Also,
C. Money to pay wages is also classified as working capital because wages are a current liability. The money set aside for wages is part of the business's short-term obligations and is necessary for the daily operation of the business.

And lastly,
D. Semi-finished goods are classified as working capital because they represent inventory that is in the process of being converted into finished products. Inventory is a key component of working capital as it can be converted into cash once sold.

A. Handling situations of abundant resources.
B. Studying the relationship between ends and means.
C. The establishment of economic laws
D. The allocation of scarce resources between alternative ends.
The correct answer is Option D. the allocation of scarce resources between alternative ends.



An economic system primarily deals with how to allocate limited or scarce resources to meet the needs and wants of a society. Since resources are finite and human desires are infinite, an economic system must decide the most efficient way to distribute these resources among various possible uses, often referred to as alternative ends.

On the other hand,
A. Handling situations of abundant resources: This is incorrect because economics primarily concerns itself with the scarcity of resources. If resources were abundant, there would be no need for an economic system to allocate them.

Also,
B. Studying the relationship between ends and means: While economics does involve analyzing the relationship between goals (ends) and the resources (means) available to achieve them, this is more of a general description of what economics as a field of study entails, rather than the primary concern of an economic system.

And lastly,
C. The establishment of economic laws: This is also incorrect. While economic laws (like supply and demand) are important, the main function of an economic system is the practical application of these laws to allocate scarce resources, not just the establishment of the laws themselves.

A. 7
B. 7.5
C. 8
D. 8.5
The correct answer is Option A. 7



To find the median of a data set, you follow these steps: Arrange the numbers in ascending order: The given marks are: 9, 8, 7, 5, 7, 6, 8, 9, 7. Arranged in ascending order: 5, 6, 7, 7, 7, 8, 8, 9, 9. Identify the median: Since there are 9 numbers (an odd number), the median is the middle value, which is the 5th number in the arranged list. In this case, the 5th number is 7.

On the other hand,
Option B: 7.5 - This would be the median if the total number of data points was even, where the median is the average of the two middle numbers. However, since we have 9 data points (an odd number), 7.5 is incorrect.

Also,
Option C: 8 - This is not the middle value of the arranged list, so it cannot be the median.

And lastly,
Option D: 8.5 - Like 7.5, this could only be the median if it was the average of the two middle values, which does not apply here.
6.

The demand for a factor input as a result of the demand for its output is known as

A. Complementary demand.
B. Competitive demand
C. Derived demand
D. Market demand.
The correct answer is Option C. derived demand.



Derived Demand: This refers to the demand for a factor input (like labor, raw materials, or machinery) that arises from the demand for the output that the input helps to produce. For example, the demand for steel is derived from the demand for cars, because steel is needed to produce cars.

On the other hand,
A. Complementary Demand: This refers to the demand for a good that complements another good. For example, the demand for printers may be related to the demand for ink cartridges. This is different from derived demand, where the demand for the input is directly linked to the demand for the final product.

Also,
B. Competitive Demand: This occurs when two or more goods serve as substitutes for each other. For example, the demand for tea might be in competition with the demand for coffee. This concept relates to goods that compete in the market rather than the demand for inputs used to produce an output.

And lastly,
D. Market Demand: This is the total demand for a product or service in the market, considering all consumers. It doesn't specifically address the demand for inputs based on the demand for outputs, but rather the overall demand in the market.
7.

If the demand for a commodity remains constant as price increases, the commodity is said to be

A. Price elastic
B. Unit price elastic.
C. Price inelastic
D. Perfectly price inelastic
The correct answer is Option D. perfectly price inelastic.



Correct Answer: D. Perfectly price inelastic When the demand for a commodity remains constant despite changes in price, the commodity is said to be perfectly price inelastic. This means that the quantity demanded does not change at all, no matter how much the price increases or decreases. In economic terms, the price elasticity of demand (PED) is zero.

On the other hand,
A. Price elastic: If a commodity is price elastic, it means that the quantity demanded changes significantly in response to price changes. If the price goes up, demand typically decreases, and if the price goes down, demand increases. This option is incorrect because price elasticity involves a change in demand, not a constant demand.

Also,
B. Unit price elastic: Unit price elasticity means that the percentage change in quantity demanded is exactly equal to the percentage change in price. For example, if the price increases by 10%, the demand decreases by 10%. This option is incorrect because, under unit price elasticity, demand would change in response to price changes.

And lastly,
C. Price inelastic: While "price inelastic" implies that demand does not change much with price changes, it does not imply that demand remains completely constant. In price inelastic demand, there is still some change, albeit small, in the quantity demanded when the price changes. This option is incorrect because it does not describe a situation where demand remains perfectly constant.
8.

When more of the tax on a product is borne by the buyer than the seller, the commodity involved has

A. Elastic demand.
B. Fairly inelastic demand.
C. Perfectly elastic demand.
D. Perfectly inelastic demand.
The correct answer is Option B. fairly inelastic demand.



Fairly Inelastic Demand (Correct Answer): When the demand for a product is fairly inelastic, consumers are less sensitive to price changes. This means that even if the price of the product increases (due to a tax, for example), the quantity demanded doesn't decrease significantly. As a result, buyers end up bearing more of the tax burden because they continue to purchase the product despite the higher price.

On the other hand,
A. Elastic Demand: If a product has elastic demand, consumers are very sensitive to price changes. If the price increases (due to a tax), the quantity demanded would decrease significantly, leading to a larger portion of the tax burden being borne by the seller. This is the opposite of what happens when the buyer bears more of the tax burden.

Also,
C. Perfectly Elastic Demand: In a perfectly elastic demand situation, even a tiny increase in price would cause the quantity demanded to drop to zero. Here, the seller would bear the entire tax burden because the buyer would stop purchasing the product entirely if the price increases.

And lastly,
D. Perfectly Inelastic Demand: With perfectly inelastic demand, the quantity demanded remains constant regardless of price changes. Theoretically, the buyer would bear all of the tax burden because their demand doesn’t change at all with price changes. However, the term "fairly inelastic demand" in the context of the question suggests a situation where the demand is somewhat inelastic but not perfectly so, making option B more appropriate for the scenario described.
9.

A fall in the price of a normal commodity which has elastic demand will result in

A. A fall in quantity demanded.
B. An increase in revenue.
C. A fall in demand.
D. A decrease in revenue.
The correct answer is Option D. a decrease in revenue.



Explanation: Elastic demand means that the quantity demanded of a commodity is very sensitive to changes in its price. Specifically, when the demand is elastic, a small decrease in price leads to a relatively larger increase in the quantity demanded. Revenue is calculated as the price of the commodity multiplied by the quantity sold (Revenue = Price × Quantity). Why D. a decrease in revenue is correct: When the price of a normal commodity with elastic demand falls, the quantity demanded increases significantly. However, because the price has decreased and the demand is elastic, the percentage decrease in price outweighs the percentage increase in quantity demanded. This results in a lower total revenue.

On the other hand,
A. a fall in quantity demanded: This is incorrect because with elastic demand, a fall in price leads to an increase, not a decrease, in quantity demanded.

Also,
B. an increase in revenue: This is incorrect because, in the case of elastic demand, a price decrease leads to a decrease in revenue, not an increase. The increase in quantity demanded does not compensate enough for the lower price.

And lastly,
C. a fall in demand: This is incorrect because "demand" refers to the overall relationship between price and quantity demanded. A price decrease affects the quantity demanded, not the overall demand curve, which shifts only due to changes in factors like consumer preferences, income, etc.

A. Complementary supply.
B. Joint supply.
C. Composite supply.

Composite Supply: This refers to a situation where different goods can be used as substitutes for each other to fulfill the same need or purpose. In this case, a candle, lantern, and gas lamp all serve the purpose of providing light, meaning they are interchangeable and meet the same need. Therefore, they form a composite supply.

On the other hand,
A. Complementary Supply: This is incorrect because complementary goods are those that are used together to satisfy a particular need, like a printer and ink. In this scenario, a candle, lantern, and gas lamp are not used together but rather as substitutes for each other.

Also,
B. Joint Supply: This is incorrect because joint supply occurs when the production of one good automatically leads to the production of another good. For example, beef and leather are joint products. The items listed (candle, lantern, and gas lamp) are not produced together but are independent of each other.

And lastly,
D. Abnormal Supply: This is incorrect because abnormal supply refers to a situation where the supply of a product does not follow the normal supply rules (e.g., due to unnatural conditions like a crisis). The supply of light using a candle, lantern, and gas lamp does not fall into this category.

A. Supply of kerosene will fall.

Since kerosene is also obtained from the same refining process, an increase in crude oil refining to produce more petrol would naturally lead to an increase in kerosene production as well.

However, an increase in the supply of petrol does not necessarily indicate that the cost of crude oil production has increased. The cost is more influenced by factors such as extraction, global demand, and geopolitical events, rather than the supply of end products like petrol and kerosene.

And lastly,
Option D (Incorrect): The supply of kerosene will not remain unchanged if the supply of petrol increases. Since both are products of the same refining process, an increase in petrol supply is likely to also increase the supply of kerosene.

If the government stops subsidy on cocoa production, the supply curve of cocoa will

A. Shift to the right
B. Become vertical
C. Shift to the left.

The correct answer is Option C. shift to the left.



C. shift to the left: This is the correct answer because if the government stops providing subsidies for cocoa production, the cost of production will increase for producers. As a result, some producers may reduce their production or exit the market altogether. This reduction in production leads to a decrease in the overall supply of cocoa, causing the supply curve to shift to the left.

On the other hand,
A. shift to the right: This option is incorrect because a shift to the right would indicate an increase in supply, which would occur if production costs decreased or if there were technological improvements, not if subsidies were removed.

B. become vertical: This option is incorrect because a vertical supply curve suggests that the quantity supplied is completely unresponsive to price changes (perfectly inelastic supply), which is not a typical outcome of removing a subsidy.

And lastly,
D. remain unchanged: This option is incorrect because removing a subsidy would generally affect the cost of production, leading to a change in the supply curve. The supply curve would not remain unchanged in such a scenario.
13.

When the marginal utility from the consumption of commodity X (MUX) is greater than the price of commodity X (PX), a rational consumer should

A. Consume more of commodity X.
B. Reduce consumption of commodity X
C. Consume the same quantity of commodity X.
D. Strive to remain in disequilibrium
The correct answer is Option A. consume more of commodity X.



Option A: Consume more of commodity X. This is the correct answer. When the marginal utility of a commodity (MUX) is greater than its price (PX), it means that the consumer derives more satisfaction or utility from consuming an additional unit of the commodity than what they have to pay for it. A rational consumer will continue to consume more of commodity X until the marginal utility equals the price (MUX = PX). At this point, the consumer maximizes their total utility, and the consumption becomes optimal.

On the other hand,
Option B: Reduce consumption of commodity X. This option is incorrect because if MUX > PX, reducing consumption would decrease the total utility. The consumer is getting more utility than the cost, so reducing consumption would not be rational.

Also,
Option C: Consume the same quantity of commodity X. This option is incorrect because if MUX > PX, maintaining the current level of consumption means the consumer is not maximizing their utility. The consumer should consume more until MUX = PX.

And lastly,
Option D: Strive to remain in disequilibrium. This option is incorrect because a rational consumer seeks to maximize utility and achieve equilibrium (MUX = PX). Deliberately staying in disequilibrium would mean not optimizing utility, which contradicts rational consumer behavior.

When the demand for a commodity increases while supply remains unchanged, the equilibrium price and quantity will

A. Remain constant
B. Decrease
C. Increase
D. Turn negative
The correct answer is Option C. increase.



Increase in Equilibrium Price and Quantity: When the demand for a commodity increases while the supply remains unchanged, consumers are willing to pay more for the same quantity of the commodity. This higher demand leads to an increase in the equilibrium price. Additionally, the higher price encourages producers to supply more of the commodity, leading to an increase in equilibrium quantity.

On the other hand,
A. Remain constant: This option is incorrect because an increase in demand with unchanged supply does not lead to a constant equilibrium price and quantity. Instead, the price will rise due to higher demand, and the quantity will also increase as suppliers respond to the higher price.

Also,
B. Decrease: This option is incorrect because an increase in demand with a constant supply results in a higher price and quantity, not a decrease. The equilibrium price and quantity both increase as the market adjusts to the new demand levels.

And lastly,
D. Turn negative: This option is incorrect because the concept of equilibrium price and quantity turning negative does not apply in this context. Prices and quantities in a market economy are generally positive values, and an increase in demand does not cause them to turn negative.

The fixing of maximum prices by government is mainly on

A. Inferior goods.
B. Luxury goods
C. Imported capital goods.

Maximum prices are price ceilings set by the government to ensure that essential goods remain affordable for consumers, especially during times of economic hardship or market fluctuations. This intervention typically targets goods that are crucial for the basic needs and well-being of the population, such as food staples, fuel, and healthcare products. By setting a maximum price, the government aims to prevent price gouging and ensure accessibility.

On the other hand,

Also,
B. Luxury goods: Luxury goods are not typically subject to maximum price regulations because they are not considered essential for survival or basic needs. Price ceilings on luxury goods would be less common since these items are not as critical to the general population.

And lastly,
C. Imported capital goods: Capital goods are assets used in the production of other goods and services, such as machinery and equipment. These are not usually subject to price ceilings because they are not consumer goods and their prices are less likely to directly impact the affordability of essential goods and services for the general population.
16.

At what point on the total product curve shown below will marginal product be negative?

A. P
B. Q
C. R
D. S
The correct answer is Option A. P.



Marginal Product (MP) is the additional output produced by adding one more unit of input, holding other inputs constant. It is the slope of the Total Product (TP) curve. When the Total Product curve is increasing at an increasing rate, the Marginal Product is positive and increasing. When the Total Product curve is increasing at a decreasing rate, the Marginal Product is positive but decreasing. When the Total Product curve reaches its maximum point (i.e., it flattens out), the Marginal Product is zero. When the Total Product curve starts to decline, the Marginal Product becomes negative. This is because the additional unit of input is actually causing a reduction in output. Option A (P) is the point where the Total Product curve starts to decline, indicating that the Marginal Product is negative. At this point, adding more units of input decreases the total output.

On the other hand,
Option B (Q) is likely the point where the Total Product curve is at its maximum or still increasing, meaning the Marginal Product is zero or positive.

Also,
Option C (R) is before the point where the Total Product starts to decline. Here, the Marginal Product is still positive.

And lastly,
Option D (S) is beyond the point where the Total Product curve starts declining. At this point, the Marginal Product would still be negative, but the question is asking for the point at which it first becomes negative.

19.

If a firm retrenches some of its factory workers, its

A. Fixed cost is likely to increase
B. Marginal cost may increase
C. Average fixed cost may increase
D. Variable cost will decline
The correct answer is Option C. average fixed cost may increase.



C. Average fixed cost may increase: This is the correct answer. Average fixed cost (AFC) is calculated by dividing total fixed costs by the number of units produced. If the firm retrenches workers and consequently produces fewer units, the fixed costs are spread over a smaller number of units, leading to an increase in average fixed cost.

On the other hand,
A. Fixed cost is likely to increase: This option is incorrect because retrenching factory workers would typically lead to a decrease in fixed costs, not an increase. Fixed costs are those that do not change with the level of output, such as salaries of permanent employees. When workers are retrenched, these fixed costs generally go down.

Also,
B. Marginal cost may increase: This option is incorrect because retrenching workers typically reduces production capacity, which may reduce output. Marginal cost is the cost of producing one additional unit of output. If production is reduced, the marginal cost of producing additional units would not be directly impacted by retrenching workers. Instead, it might become relevant if the firm needs to hire additional workers or invest in capital to increase production later.

And lastly,
D. Variable cost will decline: This option is incorrect because variable costs change with the level of production. If the firm retrenches workers, it might reduce production, which could lead to a decrease in variable costs. However, the primary effect of retrenching workers is on fixed costs, not directly on variable costs.
20.

A monopolist may enjoy abnormal profit only if its

A. Marginal cost exceeds marginal, revenue
B. Demand curve is perfectly elastic
C. Price exceeds average total cost.
D, Expenditure on advertisement increases
The correct answer is Option C. price exceeds average total cost.



Price exceeds average total cost: For a monopolist to enjoy abnormal (or economic) profit, the price charged for the good or service must be greater than the average total cost (ATC) of producing it. This means that the revenue per unit (price) is higher than the cost per unit (ATC), leading to positive economic profit.

On the other hand,
Marginal cost exceeds marginal revenue: If marginal cost (MC) exceeds marginal revenue (MR), the firm is not in a profit-maximizing position. In this case, the monopolist would be producing too much and would actually be reducing profits or increasing losses. Profit maximization occurs where MC equals MR.

Also,
Demand curve is perfectly elastic: A perfectly elastic demand curve (horizontal line) implies that the price remains constant regardless of the quantity sold. In such a situation, the firm is a price taker (like in perfect competition) rather than a monopolist. Monopolists typically face a downward-sloping demand curve.

And lastly,
Expenditure on advertisement increases: Increasing expenditure on advertising does not guarantee abnormal profit. While advertising can affect demand, the key determinant of abnormal profit is whether the price exceeds average total cost. Advertising affects demand and can influence sales, but it does not directly affect the cost structure or the relationship between price and ATC.
21.

The average revenue curve of a firm in a perfect market is the same as the

A. Supply curve of the firm.
B. Demand curve of the firm
C. Total revenue curve of the firm
D. Average cost curve of the firm
The correct answer is Option A. supply curve of the firm.



Why Option A is Correct: Supply Curve Relationship: In a perfectly competitive market, the firm's supply curve is derived from its marginal cost (MC) curve above the average variable cost (AVC). The AR curve (which equals the price) is constant, and firms decide their output based on where the price equals their MC. Therefore, the supply curve of the firm in a perfectly competitive market coincides with the AR curve above the AVC.

On the other hand,
B. Demand Curve of the Firm: In a perfectly competitive market, the demand curve faced by an individual firm is perfectly elastic (horizontal) at the market price, which is the same as the AR curve. However, the term "demand curve" generally refers to the market demand curve, not the firm's individual demand curve.

Also,
C. Total Revenue Curve of the Firm: The total revenue (TR) curve represents the total revenue generated from selling different quantities of output. In a perfect market, the TR curve is a straight line starting from the origin and increasing at a constant rate (since TR = Price × Quantity). While the average revenue is the total revenue divided by quantity, it does not directly represent the TR curve.

And lastly,
D. Average Cost Curve of the Firm: The average cost (AC) curve represents the per-unit cost of production, which is unrelated to the revenue curves. The AC curve indicates how costs change with output, while the AR curve relates to revenue.
22.

Resources are pooled for the mutual benefit of its members mainly in a

A. Joint stock company
B. Partnership
C. Private company
D. Cooperative society
The correct answer is Option D. cooperative society



Cooperative Society: A cooperative society is an organization where resources are pooled together by its members to benefit all members collectively. The primary aim is mutual benefit, and members often have equal voting rights regardless of their level of investment.

On the other hand,
Joint Stock Company: A joint stock company is a type of business entity where shares of the company's stock can be bought and sold by shareholders. The main goal is to generate profit for shareholders, not necessarily to pool resources for mutual benefit in the way a cooperative does.

Also,
Partnership: A partnership is a business arrangement where two or more individuals share ownership and the responsibilities of the business. While partners do pool resources, the focus is often on profit and shared liability, rather than mutual benefit in the way a cooperative society emphasizes.

And lastly,
Private Company: A private company is a business entity where shares are not offered to the general public but are owned by a small group of investors. The goal is usually to generate profit and serve its shareholders, rather than focusing on pooling resources for mutual benefit as in a cooperative society.

A. It is formed by a minimum of seven members

Also,
C. its shares are easily transferable: While the ease of share transfer can affect the liquidity and trading of the company's shares, it does not directly contribute to the company's perpetual existence. Perpetual existence is a feature of its corporate status, not share transferability.

And lastly,
D. there is a higher degree of specialization: Specialization within the company might enhance its efficiency and effectiveness, but it does not influence the company's ability to exist perpetually. Perpetual existence is a characteristic of its corporate nature, not its level of specialization.

The correct answer is Option B. population increases much faster than food supply



Malthusian Theory of Population: The Malthusian theory, proposed by Thomas Malthus, suggests that populations tend to grow exponentially (i.e., at a faster rate) while food supply grows at an arithmetic rate (i.e., at a slower, linear rate). This imbalance leads to overpopulation, which Malthus believed would result in famine, disease, and other forms of population control to restore balance. Why Option B is Correct: B. population increases much faster than food supply: This option accurately represents the Malthusian theory, which posits that population growth outpaces the growth of food supply, leading to a potential crisis where resources become insufficient to support the population.

On the other hand,
A. both population and food supply increase at the same rate: This option contradicts the Malthusian theory. If population and food supply increase at the same rate, there would be no imbalance, and the theory's prediction of crisis would not occur.

Also,
C. the size of the population and available resources are equal: This scenario implies a balance between population and resources, which is not in line with the Malthusian theory that predicts an imbalance due to differential growth rates.

And lastly,
D. food supply increases much faster than population growth: This option contradicts the Malthusian theory as well, which asserts that food supply grows slower than population. If food supply increased faster, it would counteract the crisis predicted by the theory.

B. Only spending on final goods arc considered.
C. Proper accounting standards must be used

In the expenditure approach to calculating Gross Domestic Product (GDP), the goal is to measure the total expenditure on final goods and services within an economy. Final goods are those that are purchased by the end user and are not used in the production of other goods.

On the other hand,
A. Value added method is used: The value added method is another approach to calculating GDP and helps avoid double counting by only considering the value added at each stage of production. However, this does not directly address the expenditure approach.

Also,
C. Proper accounting standards must be used: While proper accounting standards are important for accurate financial reporting, they do not specifically resolve the issue of double counting in the expenditure approach.

And lastly,
D. Spending on intermediate goods and inputs are considered: Including spending on intermediate goods and inputs would lead to double counting. To accurately measure GDP using the expenditure approach, only the final goods and services are counted.

And lastly,
D. Positively related: This implies that as the price level increases, the value of money also increases, which is contrary to the inverse relationship. The value of money actually decreases when the price level increases.
