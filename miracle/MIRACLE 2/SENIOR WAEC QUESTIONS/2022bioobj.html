In each of the following sentences, there is one underlined word and one gap. From the list of words lettered A to D, choose the one that is most nearly opposite in meaning to the underlined word and that will at the same time, correctly fill the gap in the sentence.

Which of the following statements about living things is correct?

A. Animals respire using carbon (IV) oxide as a raw material
B. Growth in plants is limited after some time
C. Most plants respond to stimulus slowly
D. Higher animals can reproduce asexually
The correct answer is Option C. Most plants respond to stimulus slowly.



**C. Most plants respond to stimulus slowly**: This statement is generally true. Plants do respond to stimuli such as light, gravity, and touch, but they typically do so at a much slower rate compared to animals. Given these explanations, **the correct answer is actually C. Most plants respond to stimulus slowly**. This is a true and accurate statement about living things. The correct statement should be: **Correct Answer: C. Most plants respond to stimulus slowly**

On the other hand,
**A. Animals respire using carbon (IV) oxide as a raw material**: This statement is incorrect. Animals respire using oxygen (O₂) as a raw material to produce energy, releasing carbon dioxide (CO₂) as a waste product.

Also,
**B. Growth in plants is limited after some time**: This statement is also incorrect. Unlike animals, many plants exhibit indeterminate growth, meaning they can continue to grow throughout their lifespan, given favorable conditions.

And lastly,
**D. Higher animals can reproduce asexually**: This statement is incorrect. Higher animals, such as mammals, typically reproduce sexually. Asexual reproduction is more common in simpler organisms like bacteria, certain plants, and some lower animal species (e.g., starfish, some lizards).

The organelles in cells which are also referred to as suicidal bags are the

A. mitochondria
B. lysosomes
C. ribosomes
D. nuclei
The correct answer is Option B. lysosomes.



**Lysosomes** are referred to as "suicidal bags" because they contain digestive enzymes capable of breaking down all types of biological polymers—proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, and lipids. If the lysosome membrane were to rupture, these enzymes would be released into the cell, leading to self-digestion and cell death. **Lysosomes (B)**: These contain hydrolytic enzymes for digestion and are involved in breaking down waste materials and cellular debris.

On the other hand,
**Mitochondria (A)**: Known as the powerhouse of the cell, mitochondria generate the cell's energy by producing ATP through cellular respiration.

Also,
**Ribosomes (C)**: These are the sites of protein synthesis, where translation of RNA into proteins occurs.

And lastly,
**Nuclei (D)**: The nucleus contains the cell’s genetic material (DNA) and controls the cell's growth and reproduction.

The process that brings about the shrinking of a Spirogyra cell when placed in a strong solution is

A. osmosis
B. autolysis
C. plasmolysis
D. diffusion
The correct answer is Option C. plasmolysis.



**Plasmolysis (C)**: This occurs when a plant cell is placed in a hypertonic solution (a strong solution with a higher solute concentration than the cell’s cytoplasm). Water leaves the cell by osmosis, causing the cell membrane to pull away from the cell wall and the cell to shrink.

On the other hand,
**Osmosis (A)**: This is the movement of water molecules through a selectively permeable membrane from a region of lower solute concentration to a region of higher solute concentration. While osmosis is related to plasmolysis, it does not specifically describe the shrinking of the cell.

Also,
**Autolysis (B)**: This is the self-digestion or self-destruction of a cell through the action of its own enzymes, particularly lysosomal enzymes. It is not related to the process of cell shrinking due to an external solution

And lastly,
**Diffusion (D)**: This is the movement of molecules from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration, but it does not specifically refer to the shrinking of cells.

The property of clayey soil that prevents it from supporting thick vegetation is its

A. composition of chemically weathered granite rocks.
B. inability to retain much water.
C. tendency of becoming waterlogged
D. porosity and low water-retention ability
The correct answer is Option B. inability to retain much water.



**C. tendency of becoming waterlogged**: Clayey soil has very small particles and poor drainage properties. When it rains or when there is irrigation, the soil tends to retain water, leading to waterlogging. Waterlogged soil creates anaerobic conditions, which can be detrimental to plant roots and inhibit the growth of thick vegetation.

On the other hand,
**A. composition of chemically weathered granite rocks**: While clay can be derived from weathered granite, this is not the property that directly affects its ability to support thick vegetation. The key issue is water retention and drainage, not the parent material of the soil.

Also,
**B. inability to retain much water**: This statement is incorrect because clayey soil actually retains a lot of water due to its fine particles and high surface area. The problem is not a lack of water retention but rather excessive water retention that leads to waterlogging.

And lastly,
**D. porosity and low water-retention ability**: Clayey soil has low porosity but high water retention ability, the opposite of what is stated here. The small pore spaces in clay soil hold water tightly, which can prevent proper drainage and aeration needed for thick vegetation growth.

Which of the following features is not an adaptation of plants to aquatic habitats?

A. Breathing roots for entry of air
B. Flowers are raised above water to attract pollinators

**B. Flowers are raised above water to attract pollinators**: This adaptation helps ensure successful pollination by keeping the reproductive structures above the water surface where pollinators can easily access them.

And lastly,
**C. Spongy tissues containing gases for buoyancy**: Aquatic plants often have air-filled spaces in their tissues (aerenchyma) that provide buoyancy, helping them stay upright and allowing efficient transport of oxygen to submerged parts.
38.

The biological association that contributes directly to succession in a community is

A. competition
B. predation
C. parasitism
D. commensalism
The correct answer is Option A. competition.



**Competition** is a biological association where organisms compete for limited resources such as food, water, space, or sunlight. In ecological succession, competition plays a crucial role as different species vie for resources in an environment. This competitive interaction can influence which species dominate at different stages of succession.

On the other hand,
**Predation** is a relationship where one organism (predator) hunts and consumes another organism (prey). While predation can affect the abundance of species, it is not a direct contributor to ecological succession in the same way competition is

Also,
**Parasitism** is a relationship where one organism (parasite) benefits at the expense of another organism (host). It can influence the health and population dynamics of species but does not drive primary ecological succession.

And lastly,
**Commensalism** is a relationship where one organism benefits while the other is neither harmed nor helped. It generally has minimal impact on ecological succession compared to competition.

B. increases the population of wild animals on the farm.
C. increases the dormancy period of some seeds

Also,
**Increases the dormancy period of some seeds (Option C)**: Burning can sometimes stimulate germination of certain seeds adapted to fire, rather than increasing dormancy.

And lastly,
**Destroys some plant pests (Option D)**: While burning may kill some pests, it also destroys beneficial organisms and disrupts the overall ecological balance of the soil.

Two tall plants were crossed and all the F, plants were tall. When the F plants were selfed, some of the F2 plants were tall while others were short. What are the possible genotypes of the original plants?

A. Tt and tt
B. TT and TT
C. tt and tt
D. TT and Tt
The correct answer is Option D. TT and Tt.



The given scenario involves a classic Mendelian genetics experiment with tall (T) and short (t) plants. 1. **First Cross (Parental Cross)**: - Two tall plants were crossed. - All F1 (first filial generation) plants were tall. This suggests that the tall phenotype (T) is dominant over the short phenotype (t). 2. **F1 Cross (Selfing of F1 plants)**: - When the F1 plants (Tt genotype) were selfed: - In the F2 (second filial generation), some plants were tall (TT or Tt) and some were short (tt). From these results, we deduce the genotypes of the original tall plants (parental generation): - The F1 plants were all tall, indicating they must have been heterozygous (Tt). - The possible genotypes of the original plants (P generation) that would lead to this outcome are: - TT (homozygous dominant) and Tt (heterozygous). Therefore, the correct answer is **D. TT and Tt**. This combination ensures that all F1 plants are tall (Tt) when crossed, and upon selfing, the segregation of alleles results in both tall (TT, Tt) and short (tt) plants in the F2 generation.

B. to determine the phenotype of an individual whose genotype is known

D. between two homozygous recessive individuals to confirm the genotypes of the offspring
The correct answer is Option C. between the progeny of parents to ascertain their external expressions.



**C. between the progeny of parents to ascertain their external expressions**: This correctly describes a test cross. It involves crossing the progeny (offspring) of an organism with an unknown genotype but a dominant phenotype with an organism that is homozygous recessive for the trait in question. This cross allows for the determination of the unknown genotype based on the phenotypic ratios of the offspring.

On the other hand,
**A. to determine the genotype of an individual whose phenotype is known**: This describes a backcross, not a test cross. In a backcross, an organism is crossed with one of its parents or an organism with a similar genotype.

Also,
**B. to determine the phenotype of an individual whose genotype is known**: This statement describes a typical cross where the genotype is already known, and the resulting offspring's phenotype is observed.

And lastly,
**D. between two homozygous recessive individuals to confirm the genotypes of the offspring**: This describes a homozygous test cross, which is a specific case of a test cross but not the general definition.

Which of the following statements about sex-linked traits is not correct?

A. Males inherit sex-linked traits from their fathers
B. Sex-linked traits are more common in males
C. Females can be carriers because they can be heterozygous for the trait
D. Males are never carriers since they only receive one sex chromosome
The correct answer is Option D. Males are never carriers since they only receive one sex chromosome.



**D. Males are never carriers since they only receive one sex chromosome**: This statement is incorrect. Males can be carriers of X-linked recessive traits. If a male inherits an X-linked recessive allele from his mother, he will express the trait because he only has one X chromosome. However, he can still pass the allele to his daughters, who would be carriers, or to his sons, who would express the trait. Therefore, the correct answer is **D. Males are never carriers since they only receive one sex chromosome** because males can indeed be carriers of X-linked recessive traits.

On the other hand,
**A. Males inherit sex-linked traits from their fathers**: This statement is generally true for X-linked traits because males inherit their X chromosome from their mother and their Y chromosome from their father. Therefore, X-linked traits are passed from mothers to sons.

Also,
**B. Sex-linked traits are more common in males**: This is generally true because males have only one X chromosome (and one Y chromosome), so any recessive allele on the X chromosome will be expressed in males, while females need to inherit two copies (homozygous recessive) to express the trait.

And lastly,
**C. Females can be carriers because they can be heterozygous for the trait**: This is true because females have two X chromosomes, and if they inherit a recessive allele for a sex-linked trait on one X chromosome and a dominant allele on the other, they will be carriers of the trait but not express it.

When two carriers of sickle cell anaemia produce an offspring, the probability of the offspring being a sickler is

A. ¾
B. ¼
C. ½
D. 2/2
The correct answer is Option B. ¼ .



When two carriers of sickle cell anaemia (genotype Ss) produce an offspring, the Punnett square can be used to determine the probabilities of different genotypes in their offspring: - Each parent can pass either an S (sickle cell allele) or an s (normal allele). - Possible genotypes of their offspring are SS, Ss, or ss. Here's the Punnett square for the cross between two carriers (Ss): ``` S s S SS Ss s Ss ss ``` From this Punnett square: - There is a 25% chance (1 out of 4) of the offspring being SS (homozygous for sickle cell anaemia). - There is a 50% chance (2 out of 4) of the offspring being Ss (a carrier like the parents). - There is a 25% chance (1 out of 4) of the offspring being ss (normal, without sickle cell anaemia). Therefore, the probability of the offspring being a sickler (having sickle cell anaemia, SS genotype) is **1/4 or 25%**. So, the correct answer is: **B. ¼**
