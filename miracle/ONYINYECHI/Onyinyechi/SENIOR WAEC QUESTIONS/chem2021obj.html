: The hydrolysis of proteins by dilute mineral acids produces

A. sucrose
B. glucose
C. amino acid
D. fatty acids
The correct answer is Option C. amino acid.



1. Hydrolysis of Proteins: - Proteins are long chains of amino acids linked together by peptide bonds. When proteins undergo hydrolysis, these peptide bonds are broken, resulting in the release of individual amino acids. 2. Dilute Mineral Acids: - The use of dilute mineral acids (such as hydrochloric acid, HCl) helps in breaking the peptide bonds in proteins through a process known as acid hydrolysis. This process ultimately produces free amino acids.

On the other hand,
Sucrose: - Sucrose is a disaccharide composed of glucose and fructose. It is not related to proteins or their hydrolysis. Sucrose is typically derived from sugarcane or sugar beets and is not a product of protein hydrolysis.

Also,
Glucose: - Glucose is a monosaccharide, a simple sugar that is a primary source of energy for cells. While glucose is a product of carbohydrate hydrolysis (such as starch or glycogen), it is not produced from the hydrolysis of proteins. Proteins do not contain glucose in their structure.

And lastly,
Fatty Acids: - Fatty acids are long hydrocarbon chains typically found in lipids (fats and oils). They are not components of proteins and therefore are not produced from the hydrolysis of proteins. The hydrolysis of lipids, not proteins, yields fatty acids and glycerol.

: The ratio of carbon atoms to hydrogen atoms in a hydrocarbon is 1:2. If its molecular mass is 56, what is its molecular formula?

A. C3H6
B. C4H8
C. C2H4
D. CH2
The correct answer is Option B. C4H8.



To determine the molecular formula of the hydrocarbon, we need to use the given information: the ratio of carbon to hydrogen atoms is 1:2, and the molecular mass is 56. First, let’s denote the number of carbon atoms as \( n \). Then the number of hydrogen atoms is \( 2n \). The molecular formula can be expressed as \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n} \). Next, we calculate the molecular mass: - The atomic mass of carbon (C) is 12. - The atomic mass of hydrogen (H) is 1. So, the molecular mass of \( \text{C}_n\text{H}_{2n} \) is: \[ 12n + 2n \cdot 1 = 14n \] We are given that the molecular mass is 56: \[ 14n = 56 \] Solving for \( n \): \[ n = \frac{56}{14} = 4 \] Thus, the molecular formula is \( \text{C}_4\text{H}_8 \). So, the correct answer is B. \( \text{C}_4\text{H}_8 \).

: Cathodic protection of metals is based on

A. standard electrode potential of hydrogen
B. its electrical conductivity.
C. nature of oxides formed
D. relative tendencies of oxidation
The correct answer is Option A. standard electrode potential of hydrogen.



A. Standard electrode potential of hydrogen Cathodic protection works by making the metal that needs protection the cathode of an electrochemical cell. This is typically done by connecting it to a more easily oxidized metal (the anode), which has a lower (more negative) standard electrode potential than the metal to be protected. The standard electrode potential of hydrogen is a reference point used in electrochemistry to compare the tendencies of different metals to be oxidized or reduced. By selecting an appropriate sacrificial anode, the metal to be protected becomes less likely to oxidize (corrode).

On the other hand,
B. Its electrical conductivity While electrical conductivity is important in the context of ensuring that there is good electrical contact between the metal and the sacrificial anode, it is not the basis for cathodic protection. Conductivity ensures the flow of electrons, but the fundamental principle relies on the relative electrode potentials, not just the ability to conduct electricity.

Also,
C. Nature of oxides formed The nature of oxides formed on metals can influence corrosion rates, but cathodic protection specifically involves preventing oxidation by making the metal the cathode. The formation of protective oxides is a different corrosion control method known as passivation.

And lastly,
D. Relative tendencies of oxidation This option is somewhat related but not as precise as the correct answer. The relative tendencies of oxidation of metals are directly determined by their standard electrode potentials. Therefore, using the standard electrode potential of hydrogen as a reference provides a more accurate and direct understanding of which metal will act as the sacrificial anode and which will be protected.

The correct answer is Option D. double decomposition.



Double decomposition (also known as double displacement or metathesis) is a chemical reaction where two compounds react to form two new compounds by the exchange of ions. In the context of preparing insoluble salts, this method involves mixing solutions of two soluble salts that contain ions which can form an insoluble salt upon reaction. The insoluble salt then precipitates out of solution. Double decomposition is commonly used to prepare many insoluble salts in the laboratory.

: What number of moles of oxygen would exert a pressure of 10 atm at 320 K in an 8.2 dm3 cylinder? [R = 0.082 atm dm3 mol-1 K-1]

A.0.32
B. 1.56
C. 3.13
D. 31.25
The correct answer is Option C. 3.13.



To find the number of moles of oxygen that would exert a pressure of 10 atm in an 8.2 dm³ cylinder at 320 K, we can use the ideal gas law equation: \[ PV = nRT \] Where: - \( P \) is the pressure of the gas (in atm), - \( V \) is the volume of the gas (in dm³), - \( n \) is the number of moles of the gas, - \( R \) is the ideal gas constant (\( 0.082 \, \text{atm} \cdot \text{dm}³ \cdot \text{mol}^{-1} \cdot \text{K}^{-1} \)), - \( T \) is the temperature (in Kelvin). First, convert the volume to liters (since \( 1 \, \text{dm}³ = 1 \, \text{liter} \)): \[ V = 8.2 \, \text{dm}³ = 8.2 \, \text{liters} \] Now, rearrange the ideal gas law to solve for \( n \): \[ n = \frac{PV}{RT} \] Substitute the given values: - \( P = 10 \, \text{atm} \) - \( V = 8.2 \, \text{liters} = 8.2 \, \text{dm}³ \) - \( R = 0.082 \, \text{atm} \cdot \text{dm}³ \cdot \text{mol}^{-1} \cdot \text{K}^{-1} \) - \( T = 320 \, \text{K} \) Calculate \( n \): \[ n = \frac{10 \times 8.2}{0.082 \times 320} \] \[ n = \frac{82}{26.24} \] \[ n \approx 3.13 \] Therefore, the number of moles of oxygen that would exert a pressure of 10 atm in an 8.2 dm³ cylinder at 320 K is approximately \( \mathbf{3.13} \) moles. Thus, the correct answer is \( \mathbf{C. 3.13} \).

Also,

And lastly,
D. Low Solubility - Low solubility means that a substance does not dissolve easily in water. While low solubility might be advantageous for certain applications, it is not the key property for a drying agent. In fact, many effective drying agents are quite soluble in water (e.g., calcium chloride). The primary function of a drying agent is to absorb moisture, regardless of its solubility.

: Under which conditions of pressure (P) and temperature (T) would the volume of an inflated balloon increase? When

A. both T and P are increased
B. both T and P are decreased
C. T is increased and P is decreased
D. T is decreased and P is increased
The correct answer is Option C. T is increased and P is decreased.



The correct answer is C. T is increased and P is decreased. This scenario follows the ideal gas law, which states: \[ PV = nRT \] where \( P \) is pressure, \( V \) is volume, \( n \) is the number of moles of gas, \( R \) is the ideal gas constant, and \( T \) is temperature in Kelvin. According to the ideal gas law, if temperature \( T \) is increased (while keeping pressure \( P \) constant or decreasing it) for a fixed amount of gas (\( n \)), the volume \( V \) of the gas will increase. This is because increasing temperature increases the average kinetic energy of gas molecules, causing them to move faster and thus occupy a larger volume. Therefore, when \( T \) is increased and \( P \) is decreased, the volume \( V \) of the inflated balloon will increase. This corresponds to option C as the correct answer.

:The collision between ideal gas molecules are considered to be perfectly elastic because

A. they collide without losing energy.
B.they collide without losing energy.
C. their average kinetic energy is variable.
D. the distance between them is large compared to their sizes.
The correct answer is Option A. they collide without losing energy.



The correct answer is A. they collide without losing energy. In an ideal gas, the molecules are considered to have perfectly elastic collisions because when they collide: - Kinetic energy is conserved: The total kinetic energy of the molecules before and after collision remains the same. - Momentum is conserved: The total momentum of the molecules before and after collision is also conserved. This means that when two ideal gas molecules collide, they do not lose any kinetic energy to other forms (such as heat or potential energy). Instead, they transfer kinetic energy between themselves during collision, but the total amount of kinetic energy in the system remains constant. Option A correctly states that ideal gas molecules collide without losing energy, which reflects the principle of perfectly elastic collisions in ideal gases.

: Which of the following scientists discovered the electron?

A. Joseph J. Thompson
B. James Chadwick
C. Amedeo Avogadro
D. Ernest Rutherford
The correct answer is Option A. Joseph J. Thompson.



Joseph J. Thomson (also spelled "Thomson") is credited with the discovery of the electron. He made this discovery in 1897 through his experiments with cathode rays. Thomson demonstrated that cathode rays were composed of negatively charged particles, which he called "corpuscles" (later renamed electrons). His work showed that these particles were much smaller than atoms and were a fundamental component of all atoms.

On the other hand,
James Chadwick discovered the neutron in 1932. The neutron is a neutral particle found in the nucleus of the atom, alongside protons. His discovery helped to explain the missing mass in the atomic nucleus and led to a better understanding of atomic structure.

Also,
Amedeo Avogadro is known for Avogadro's law, which states that equal volumes of gases, at the same temperature and pressure, contain an equal number of molecules. He is also known for the Avogadro constant, which is the number of constituent particles (usually atoms or molecules) in one mole of a substance.

And lastly,
Ernest Rutherford is famous for his gold foil experiment, which led to the discovery of the atomic nucleus. In 1911, Rutherford proposed the nuclear model of the atom, where a dense, positively charged nucleus is surrounded by electrons. He also identified and named the proton in 1917.
