1: The old man occupied the rat-infested building in the slum.

A. Built
B. Inhabited
C. Rented
D. Vacated
The correct answer is Option D. Vacated.



In the sentence, "The old man occupied the rat-infested building," the word "occupied" means to take possession or control of something, typically a place. The opposite of "occupied" in this context would be "vacated," which means to leave or abandon a place. Vacated (option D): This is the correct answer because it means to leave or evacuate a place, which is the opposite action to occupying it.

On the other hand,
Built (option A): This does not directly relate to the action of taking possession of a place. Building is the act of constructing something, not occupying it.

Also,
Inhabited (option B): This means to live in or occupy as a place of residence, which is similar in meaning to "occupied" but not opposite.

And lastly,
Rented (option C): This means to use something for a period of time in exchange for payment. While it involves occupancy, it doesn't imply taking possession or control as strongly as "occupied."

The correct answer is Option A. A metaphor.

For example, saying "Time is a thief" compares time to a thief, implying that time takes things away stealthily.

On the other hand,

Allusion (Option D): An allusion is a reference to another text, person, or historical event. It is not a comparison between two unlike things.
