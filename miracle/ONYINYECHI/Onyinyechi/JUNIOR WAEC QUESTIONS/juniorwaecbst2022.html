: A component of hydraulic machine that controls the fluid direction, pressure and flow rate is.

A. Actuator
B. Pipe
C. Pump
D. Reservoir
E. Valve
The correct answer is Option E. Valve



**Valve (E):** - A valve is a component in hydraulic systems that controls the direction, pressure, and flow rate of the hydraulic fluid. Valves can be directional control valves, pressure control valves, or flow control valves, each serving a specific function in managing the hydraulic fluid within the system.

On the other hand,
**Actuator (A):** - An actuator converts the hydraulic energy into mechanical energy to perform work, such as moving a load. It does not control the fluid's direction, pressure, or flow rate.

Also,
**Pipe (B):** - Pipes are conduits that transport hydraulic fluid from one component to another. They do not control the direction, pressure, or flow rate of the fluid.

Also,
**Pump (C):** - A pump moves the hydraulic fluid from the reservoir into the system, creating flow and pressure. However, it does not directly control the fluid's direction, pressure, or flow rate once it is in the system.

And lastly,
**Reservoir (D):** - The reservoir stores the hydraulic fluid and supplies it to the pump. It does not control the fluid's direction, pressure, or flow rate.

: What is the ratio of a driving gear with 20 teeth that is driven by 5 teeth gear?.

A. 1:4
B. 1:5
C. 1:10
D. 4:1
E. 5:1
The correct answer is Option A. 1:4.



To determine the ratio of the driving gear to the driven gear, we need to compare the number of teeth on each gear. Given: - Driving gear: 20 teeth - Driven gear: 5 teeth The gear ratio is calculated as: \[ \text{Gear Ratio} = \frac{\text{Number of teeth on driving gear}}{\text{Number of teeth on driven gear}} \] So, \[ \text{Gear Ratio} = \frac{20}{5} = 4 \] This means the driving gear has 4 times the number of teeth as the driven gear. Therefore, the ratio is 4:1. The correct answer is **D. 4:1**.

On the other hand,
**A. 1:4**: This would imply that the driving gear has fewer teeth than the driven gear, which is not the case here.

Also,
**B. 1:5**: This would also imply that the driving gear has fewer teeth than the driven gear, which is incorrect.

Also,
**C. 1:10**: This would imply an even greater discrepancy, where the driving gear has significantly fewer teeth than the driven gear, which is incorrect.

And lastly,
**E. 5:1**: This would imply that the driving gear has five times the number of teeth of the driven gear, which is incorrect since it actually has four times the number of teeth.

: The ability of a material to resist breaking is.

A.Brittleness
B.Ductility
C. Elasticity
D. Hardness
E. Toughness
The correct answer is Option E. Toughness.



**Toughness (E)**: Toughness is the ability of a material to absorb energy and plastically deform without fracturing. It combines both strength and ductility, indicating how well a material can resist breaking under impact or sudden stress.

On the other hand,
**Brittleness (A)**: Brittleness refers to a material's tendency to fracture or break without significant deformation when subjected to stress. It does not measure the ability to resist breaking but rather how a material behaves when it breaks suddenly.

Also,
**Ductility (B)**: Ductility is the ability of a material to undergo significant plastic deformation before rupture or breaking. It relates to how much a material can be stretched or drawn into a wire.

Also,
**Elasticity (C)**: Elasticity refers to the ability of a material to return to its original shape after deformation when the applied stress is removed. It does not directly measure resistance to breaking.

And lastly,
**Hardness (D)**: Hardness measures a material's resistance to local deformation (e.g., indentation or scratching) rather than its ability to resist breaking under stress.
