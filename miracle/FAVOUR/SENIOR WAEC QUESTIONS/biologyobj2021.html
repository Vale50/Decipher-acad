Which of the following organelles is common to both plant and animal cells?

A. Cellulose cell wall
B. Chlorophyll
C. Cell membrane
D. Large vacuole
The correct is Option C. Cell membrane.



**Cell membrane**: This organelle is present in both plant and animal cells. It is a thin, flexible barrier that surrounds the cell and controls the movement of substances in and out of the cell. It plays a crucial role in protecting the cell and maintaining its internal environment.

On the other hand,
**Cellulose cell wall**: This structure is found only in plant cells. It provides additional support and protection to the plant cell and is composed mainly of cellulose, a type of carbohydrate.

Also,
**Chlorophyll**: This pigment is found in chloroplasts, which are present only in plant cells and some algae. Chlorophyll is essential for photosynthesis, the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy.

And lastly,
**Large vacuole**: Plant cells typically have a large central vacuole that serves multiple functions, including storage, waste disposal, and maintaining turgor pressure within the cell. Animal cells may have small vacuoles, but they do not have the large central vacuole characteristic of plant cells.

A plant cell placed in a solution with a higher water potential will

A. Expand and'then shrink..
B. Enlarge and become turgid.
C. Expand and then burst.
D. Decrease in size and become flaccid.
The correct is Option D. Decrease in size and become flaccid.



**D. Decrease in size and become flaccid**: This is the correct answer. When a plant cell is placed in a hypertonic solution (higher water potential outside the cell), water moves out of the cell, causing it to shrink. As the cell loses water, it loses turgor pressure and becomes flaccid.

On the other hand,
**A. Expand and then shrink**: This would happen if the cell were placed in a hypotonic solution (lower water potential than the cell's cytoplasm). Water would initially move into the cell, causing it to expand, and then it would shrink as water moves out due to plasmolysis.

Also,
**B. Enlarge and become turgid**: This describes what happens when a cell is placed in a hypotonic solution. Water moves into the cell, causing it to swell and become turgid.

And lastly,
**C. Expand and then burst**: This extreme scenario would occur if the cell were placed in a strongly hypotonic solution where water continuously enters the cell without restriction, leading to cell lysis and bursting.

Offspring formed by sexual reproduction exhibit more variation than those formed by asexual reproduction because

A. Greater amount of DNA is involved in sexual reproduction
B. Gametes of parents have different genetic composition.
C. Genetic materials come from parents of two different species.
D. Sexual reproduction is a lengthy process
The correct is Option B. Gametes of parents have different genetic composition.



Sexual reproduction involves the combination of genetic material from two parents, each contributing a gamete (sperm and egg in animals, or pollen and ovule in plants). These gametes contain different sets of genetic information due to the process of meiosis, which includes crossing over and independent assortment. This results in offspring with a unique mix of genetic traits, increasing genetic variation

On the other hand,
**A. Greater amount of DNA is involved in sexual reproduction** - **Explanation**: The amount of DNA involved is not what causes the increased variation. Both sexual and asexual reproduction can involve substantial amounts of DNA, but the key factor in sexual reproduction is the combination and recombination of different sets of DNA from two parents.

Also,
**C. Genetic materials come from parents of two different species.** - **Explanation**: Sexual reproduction generally involves parents of the same species. Genetic material from different species (interspecies reproduction) is rare and typically results in sterile hybrids if it occurs at all. The increased variation in sexual reproduction is due to the mixing of genetic material within the same species.

And lastly,
**D. Sexual reproduction is a lengthy process** - **Explanation**: The length of the reproductive process is not directly related to the genetic variation of the offspring. The increased variation is due to the recombination of genetic material from two parents, not the duration of the process.

Detoxification of urea takes place primarily in the liver. Urea is a waste product formed from the breakdown of proteins and amino acids in the body. Which of the following hormones is suddenly secreted into the bloodstream of a frightened person?

A. Insulin.
B. Adrenaline
C. Thyroxine
D.Parathormone.
The correct is Option B. Adrenaline



When a person is frightened or experiences a sudden stressful situation, the body's sympathetic nervous system is activated, leading to the release of adrenaline (also known as epinephrine) into the bloodstream. Adrenaline is a hormone produced by the adrenal glands, which are located on top of the kidneys. Adrenaline plays a crucial role in the "fight or flight" response, which prepares the body to react quickly to perceived threats or emergencies. It causes several physiological changes, such as increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, dilation of air passages in the lungs, and increased blood flow to muscles. These changes help the person respond effectively to the stressful situation.

On the other hand,
- **A. Insulin:** Insulin is a hormone produced by the pancreas that regulates blood sugar levels by promoting the uptake of glucose into cells. It is not related to the body's response to fear or stress.

Also,
**C. Thyroxine:** Thyroxine (also known as T4) is a hormone produced by the thyroid gland that regulates metabolism. It does not have a direct role in the immediate stress response.

And lastly,
- **D. Parathormone:** Parathormone (parathyroid hormone) is produced by the parathyroid glands and regulates calcium and phosphorus levels in the blood. It is not involved in the acute stress response.

How many gametes are produced in the pollen grain of a flowering plant before fertilization?

A. 0ne
B. two
C.three
D.four
The correct is Option B. two.



In flowering plants, pollen grains are produced by the male reproductive organs (anthers) of flowers. Each pollen grain contains two haploid (n) gametes before fertilization occurs. These gametes are: 1. **Generative cell:** This is the smaller of the two cells within the pollen grain and it divides mitotically to produce two sperm cells. 2. **Tube cell:** This cell is larger and it forms the pollen tube, which delivers the sperm cells to the ovule during fertilization. So, before fertilization, a pollen grain contains two gametes: one generative cell that will divide to form two sperm cells, and one tube cell that supports the transport of these sperm cells to the female reproductive structure (ovule). Therefore, the correct answer is **B. two** gametes.

Which of the following organisms does not undergo incomplete metamorphosis?

A. Locust
B. Grasshopper
C.Butterfly
D.Cockroach
The correct is Option C.Butterfly.



Incomplete metamorphosis is a type of development characterized by three life stages: egg, nymph (or juvenile), and adult. Insects that undergo incomplete metamorphosis have nymphs that resemble adults but are smaller and lack wings. They gradually develop wings and reproductive organs through a series of molts until they reach adulthood. Butterflies undergo complete metamorphosis, not incomplete metamorphosis. They have four distinct life stages: egg, larva (caterpillar), pupa (chrysalis), and adult. The larval stage (caterpillar) looks very different from the adult butterfly and goes through a complete transformation during the pupal stage

On the other hand,
**A. Locust**: locusts undergo incomplete metamorphosis. They hatch from eggs into nymphs, which resemble adults but lack wings. They molt several times, gradually developing wings and adult features.

Also,
**B. Grasshopper**: grasshoppers undergo incomplete metamorphosis. They hatch from eggs into nymphs, which resemble adults but lack wings. They molt several times, gradually developing wings and adult features.

And lastly,
**D. Cockroach**: Cockroaches also undergo incomplete metamorphosis. They hatch from eggs into nymphs, which look like smaller versions of adults. They molt multiple times, with each stage resembling the adult more closely until reaching full adulthood.

An enzyme reaction may begin to decline when

A. The optimum temperature is attained .
B. The pH of the medium is altered.
C. There is an increase in substrate concentration.
D. The atmospheric pressure is altered.
The correct is Option B. The pH of the medium is altered.



Enzyme activity is highly sensitive to changes in pH. Each enzyme has an optimal pH range within which it operates most efficiently. Deviations from this optimal pH can lead to a decline in enzyme activity due to changes in the enzyme's structure and the ionization state of the substrate or active site residues, which are crucial for binding and catalysis.

On the other hand,
**A. The optimum temperature is attained**: When the optimum temperature is attained, enzyme activity is at its peak. Decline usually occurs if the temperature exceeds the optimum, leading to denaturation, or if it is too low, resulting in decreased kinetic energy and slower reaction rates.

Also,
**C. There is an increase in substrate concentration**: An increase in substrate concentration typically increases the rate of reaction until the enzyme becomes saturated. Once saturation is reached, the reaction rate plateaus but does not decline unless other factors such as pH or temperature are altered.

And lastly,
**D. The atmospheric pressure is altered**: Atmospheric pressure changes generally do not affect enzyme activity significantly unless the changes are extreme. Enzymes are more sensitive to changes in pH and temperature.

The process that takes place at the light stage of photosynthesis is

A. Splitting of water molecules to form hydrogen ions
B. Reduction of carbon (IV) oxide to form carbohydrate.
C. Formation of two molecules of phosphorescence acid.
D. Breaking down of ATP molecules to produce energy.
The correct is Option A. Splitting of water molecules to form hydrogen ions



During the light-dependent reactions of photosynthesis, also known as the light stage, several key processes occur: - **Splitting of water molecules (photolysis)**: Light energy is used to split water molecules into oxygen, protons (hydrogen ions), and electrons. This process generates oxygen as a byproduct and provides the electrons and protons needed for the synthesis of ATP and NADPH, which are used in the subsequent dark reactions (Calvin cycle).

On the other hand,
**B. Reduction of carbon (IV) oxide to form carbohydrate**: This process takes place during the Calvin cycle, not the light stage.

Also,
**C. Formation of two molecules of phosphorescence acid**: This is not a recognized step in photosynthesis.

And lastly,
**D. Breaking down of ATP molecules to produce energy**: ATP is produced, not broken down, during the light stage of photosynthesis. It is later used in the Calvin cycle to help form carbohydrates.

Lamarck's theory of evolution, known as Lamarckism, is based on the idea that organisms can pass on characteristics that they acquire during their lifetime to their offspring. This theory consists of several key postulates: 1. **Use and Disuse**: Organs that are frequently used become more developed, while those that are not used diminish in size or become vestigial. 2. **Inheritance of Acquired Characteristics**: Traits that an organism acquires during its lifetime due to use or disuse can be passed on to its offspring. 3. **Change in Environment**: Changes in the environment cause changes in the needs of organisms living in that environment, leading to changes in their behavior, which in turn leads to changes in their physical characteristics. **D. Survivors in a competitive community must have inherited useful traits**, however, is more aligned with Darwinian evolution, which emphasizes natural selection and the survival of the fittest, rather than the inheritance of acquired characteristics. Thus, it is not a postulate of Lamarck's theory of evolution.
