C.metres per second
D. newton
The correct answer is B. kilogram



**Newton (D):** The newton is the S.I. unit of force. It is defined as the amount of force required to accelerate a one-kilogram mass by one meter per second squared (1 N = 1 kg·m/s²).

On the other hand,

**Ampere (A):** The ampere is the S.I. unit of electric current, not force.

Also,
**Kilogram (B):** The kilogram is the S.I. unit of mass, not force.
And lastly,
**Metres per second (C):** Metres per second is a unit of velocity, not force.

Overgrazing of grassland by cattle exposes the land to?

A. agriculture.
B. erosion
C. sheets.
D. tars
The correct answer is B. erosion



**Overgrazing** occurs when cattle or other livestock feed on grassland vegetation more quickly than it can regenerate. This excessive grazing removes the vegetation cover, which plays a crucial role in protecting the soil from the direct impact of rainfall and wind. - **Erosion** is the process where soil is removed by wind or water. Without sufficient vegetation to hold the soil together and shield it, the land becomes more susceptible to erosion. Therefore, overgrazing leads to increased erosion of the topsoil, which can result in land degradation and reduced agricultural productivity.

On the other hand,
**A. agriculture**: While overgrazing can impact land used for agriculture, the direct consequence of overgrazing is not the exposure to agriculture. Agriculture refers to the cultivation of crops and rearing of animals, which is not a direct result of overgrazing.

Also,
**C. sheets**: This option doesn't make sense in the context of the question. "Sheets" could refer to sheet erosion, but erosion is already covered in option B, and the term alone is too vague to be considered correct.

And lastly,
**D. tars**: This option is not relevant in the context of overgrazing. Tars are sticky, black substances derived from organic materials, and they have no direct relation to the consequences of overgrazing.
